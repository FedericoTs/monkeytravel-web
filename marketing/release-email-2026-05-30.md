## Send strategy

**Recipient options:**

1. **Everyone confirmed in `auth.users`** (n≈121) — broadest reach. Use
   their stamped `user_metadata.locale` to pick EN/IT/ES variant.
2. **Only `email_subscribers` with `verified=true`** (n≈6) — tiny, but
   they explicitly opted in. Safest from a CAN-SPAM / GDPR perspective.
3. **Hybrid**: send to all confirmed `auth.users` BUT include a clear
   unsubscribe link + footer "you received this because you signed up
   at monkeytravel.app". Treats this as transactional/account-related,
   not pure marketing.

**Recommendation: hybrid.** The first-party signup gives you a
legitimate-interest basis for sending product updates, especially when
you frame it as an apology + product news (the "we let you down + here's
what we did" framing is well-established legitimate-interest practice).

**How to send via Resend Audiences:**

1. Create an audience: `Product updates - 2026-05-30`
2. Sync from `auth.users` via the existing
   `scripts/sync-resend-audience.mts` (already in the codebase)
3. Compose email in Resend UI with the body above
4. Set From: `MonkeyTravel <hello@monkeytravel.app>` (not noreply — this
   is a reply-friendly broadcast)
5. Set reply-to: `hello@monkeytravel.app`
6. Send to audience in batches of 50 (Resend free tier limit)

**Timing:** Tuesday or Thursday morning local time gets best open rates
for travel content. Avoid Friday (weekend mode) and Monday (inbox
backlog).
