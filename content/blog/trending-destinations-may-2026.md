# Trending Destinations for May 2026: Where to Travel This Month

May 2026 is a strong travel month. Shoulder season pricing is still in effect across Southern Europe and Southeast Asia. Domestic US destinations are hitting peak spring conditions without peak summer crowds. And several major events — from music festivals to cultural celebrations — are creating temporary spikes in demand for specific cities.

This is the first installment of MonkeyTravel's monthly trending destinations report. Every month, we analyze booking data, search trends, event calendars, and flight pricing to identify the 10 destinations with the best combination of momentum, value, and timing.

Here are the 10 trending destinations for May 2026 — 5 domestic (US) and 5 international.

---

## Trending Domestic Destinations

### 1. Nashville, Tennessee

Nashville search volume is up 34% year-over-year for May 2026. The city has evolved well beyond its country music roots into a legitimate food, nightlife, and cultural hub.

**Why it's trending now:** CMA Fest runs June 5-8, and pre-festival energy is driving May bookings as travelers look to enjoy Nashville before peak crowd season. The Gulch and East Nashville neighborhoods continue to attract younger travelers with new restaurant openings and live music seven nights a week.

**Daily budget:** $120-180 (mid-range hotel, meals, attractions).

**Best for:** Couples, friend groups, and solo travelers who want live music, strong food scenes, and walkable nightlife. Ages 25-45.

---

### 2. Asheville, North Carolina

Asheville bookings for May are up 28% compared to 2025. The city's recovery from Hurricane Helene in September 2024 is largely complete, and the community has rallied around a "rebuild and return" campaign.

**Why it's trending now:** May is Asheville's best month — wildflower season along the Blue Ridge Parkway, comfortable 70F temperatures, and the craft brewery scene operating at full capacity. The rebuilt River Arts District reopened fully in early 2026 with new studios and galleries.

**Daily budget:** $100-160.

**Best for:** Outdoor enthusiasts, craft beer lovers, and couples seeking a quieter alternative to larger cities. The Blue Ridge Parkway is one of the best spring drives in the US.

---

### 3. San Diego, California

San Diego is trending for May due to ideal weather conditions — average highs of 70F, minimal rain, and ocean water temperatures warm enough for surfing without a full wetsuit.

**Why it's trending now:** Hotel rates in May are 20-30% lower than July-August peak season. The Gaslamp Quarter and North Park food scenes continue to expand. New direct flight routes from Austin, Nashville, and Denver are making the city more accessible from mid-size markets.

**Daily budget:** $140-200.

**Best for:** Beach travelers, families, and food-focused visitors. San Diego's taco and craft beer scenes are among the best in the US. Also strong for solo travelers — the city is walkable, safe, and easy to navigate.

---

### 4. Portland, Oregon

Portland searches for May travel are up 22% year-over-year. The city offers the best of Pacific Northwest spring — blooming gardens, mild temperatures, and access to both coast and mountains within 90 minutes.

**Why it's trending now:** Portland's food cart culture has rebounded strongly after the pandemic-era contraction. The Alberta Arts District and Hawthorne neighborhoods have new openings. May avoids the June-September rain gap, offering dry days and 65-70F temperatures that are ideal for exploring on foot.

**Daily budget:** $110-170.

**Best for:** Foodies, independent travelers, and anyone interested in a city that prioritizes local culture over chain commercialism. The bookstore (Powell's) alone is worth a half-day.

---

### 5. Savannah, Georgia

Savannah is having a moment. The city appeared on multiple 2026 "best of" lists and May is the sweet spot — warm enough for the riverfront but before the oppressive summer humidity sets in.

**Why it's trending now:** The new Plant Riverside District has added a critical mass of hotels, restaurants, and event spaces to Savannah's offering. Azaleas are still blooming in May. The SCAD (Savannah College of Art and Design) influence has transformed the city's gallery and design scene.

**Daily budget:** $100-150.

**Best for:** History buffs, couples, architecture enthusiasts, and anyone who appreciates a slower pace. Savannah's historic squares and Spanish moss canopy make it one of the most photogenic cities in the US.

## Trending International Destinations

### 6. Portugal (Lisbon and Porto)

Portugal remains the best-value destination in Western Europe. May offers warm weather (70-80F), manageable tourist density, and shoulder-season pricing on flights and hotels.

**Why it's trending now:** New direct flights from several US cities launched in spring 2026. The euro remains favorable against the dollar compared to 2023-2024 highs. Portugal's gastronomy scene — particularly in Porto — continues to gain international recognition. Lisbon's startup and digital nomad ecosystem is attracting longer-stay visitors.

**Daily budget:** $80-130 (including accommodation).

**Best for:** First-time European travelers, couples, digital nomads, and budget-conscious visitors who want Western European quality at Eastern European-adjacent prices. Porto for food; Lisbon for nightlife and culture; the Algarve for beaches.

---

### 7. Japan (Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka)

Japan continues its post-pandemic tourism surge. May is one of the best months to visit — cherry blossom season has ended (reducing extreme crowds), but the weather is warm, dry, and ideal for walking.

**Why it's trending now:** The yen has stabilized but remains weak by historical standards, keeping Japan affordable for US travelers. Golden Week (April 29 - May 5) creates a brief surge, but mid-to-late May is significantly calmer. New shinkansen routes have improved access to secondary cities. Japan's food scene is unmatched in depth and value.

**Daily budget:** $100-180.

**Best for:** Food travelers, culture enthusiasts, and first-time visitors to Asia. Japan is one of the safest and most efficient countries in the world, making it ideal for solo travelers and families. Mid-May through early June is the narrow window between cherry blossoms and rainy season.

---

### 8. Morocco (Marrakech and Fez)

Morocco is trending due to strong value, unique cultural experiences, and comfortable May temperatures before the brutal summer heat.

**Why it's trending now:** May temperatures in Marrakech average 80-85F — warm but manageable, especially in the evenings. Direct flights from New York, Paris, and London keep access easy. Morocco's riad (traditional courtyard hotel) culture offers accommodation experiences that do not exist anywhere else at this price point. The Fez medina was recently named a UNESCO priority restoration site, driving cultural tourism interest.

**Daily budget:** $60-110.

**Best for:** Culture-focused travelers, photographers, couples, and adventurous eaters. Morocco offers a genuine sensory overload — the souks, the architecture, the food — that few destinations match. Not ideal for travelers who prefer highly structured or predictable environments.

---

### 9. Croatia (Dubrovnik, Split, Hvar)

Croatia searches for May are up 18% year-over-year. The country has become a top shoulder-season destination because May offers warm Mediterranean weather without July-August pricing and overcrowding.

**Why it's trending now:** Dubrovnik and Split hotels in May cost 30-40% less than in July. Water temperatures reach 68-72F — swimmable for most travelers. The island-hopping route between Split, Hvar, and Korcula is at its most accessible in May before the summer ferry schedule changes. Croatia is also benefiting from travelers seeking Mediterranean alternatives to increasingly expensive Greece and Italy.

**Daily budget:** $80-140.

**Best for:** Beach and island travelers, history enthusiasts (Diocletian's Palace, Dubrovnik walls), and couples. Croatia combines the Mediterranean aesthetic with Balkan pricing — though the gap is narrowing.

---

### 10. Colombia (Bogota, Medellin, Cartagena)

Colombia is the fastest-growing major destination in South America. May sits in a dry-weather window for most of the country, making it an optimal month for travel.

**Why it's trending now:** New direct flights from Miami, Houston, and New York have reduced travel time and cost. Medellin's transformation into a digital nomad hub continues to drive awareness. Cartagena's old walled city remains one of the most photogenic destinations in the Americas. The Colombian peso offers strong purchasing power for US dollar holders. Safety perceptions have shifted meaningfully — Bogota and Medellin are no longer fringe destinations.

**Daily budget:** $50-100.

**Best for:** Adventurous travelers, digital nomads, foodies, and budget-conscious visitors looking for a destination with genuine depth. Colombia's regional diversity — Andean highlands, Caribbean coast, coffee country, Pacific jungle — packs multiple trips into one country.

## What's Driving These Trends

Three macro factors are shaping May 2026 travel trends:

**Shoulder season value.** May sits in the gap between spring break and peak summer. Flights to Europe are 15-25% cheaper than June-August. Hotels in popular beach destinations have not yet switched to high-season rates. Travelers who are flexible on dates are finding May offers the best price-to-weather ratio of the year.

**Events and cultural moments.** Nashville's pre-CMA Fest energy, Asheville's post-recovery campaign, and Fez's UNESCO designation are creating specific demand spikes. Event-driven travel continues to grow as travelers seek experiences tied to specific moments rather than generic vacations.

**Currency and accessibility.** The weak yen keeps Japan affordable. The strong dollar makes Colombia, Morocco, and Portugal exceptional value. New airline routes — particularly from mid-size US cities — are opening destinations that previously required connections. Direct flights reduce both cost and friction, and airlines are responding to demand patterns that emerged during the post-pandemic travel surge.

## FAQ

### What is the best travel destination for May 2026?

For domestic US travel, Nashville and San Diego offer the strongest combination of weather, value, and things to do. For international travel, Portugal and Japan provide the best overall experience-to-cost ratio in May 2026.

### Is May a good month to travel to Europe?

May is one of the best months for European travel. Temperatures are comfortable (65-80F across Southern Europe), tourist crowds have not yet peaked, and shoulder-season pricing is still available for flights and hotels. Portugal, Croatia, and Greece are particularly strong in May.

### What international destinations are trending in 2026?

Japan, Portugal, Morocco, Croatia, and Colombia are the five most consistently trending international destinations in 2026. Japan benefits from the weak yen. Portugal and Croatia offer Mediterranean quality at below-average prices. Morocco and Colombia provide high-value, culturally rich experiences.

### How can I find cheap flights for May 2026?

Book 6-8 weeks in advance for the best domestic fares. For international flights, check for new route launches (often discounted) and be flexible on departure day — Tuesday and Wednesday flights are typically 15-20% cheaper than weekend departures.
