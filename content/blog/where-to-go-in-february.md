# Where to Go in February 2026: 12 Best Destinations for Winter Travel

February is the most underrated travel month on the calendar. Airfares hit their annual floor — demand is the lowest of any month after January — and yet the weather in the Southern Hemisphere is at its summer peak, Carnival turns two of the world's great cities into spectacles, dry season delivers perfect conditions across Asia and East Africa, and the Northern Lights burn at their most reliable. The problem isn't finding somewhere to go. It's choosing between genuinely excellent options.

One more thing February has going for it: Valentine's Day. Romantic destinations fill up fast in the second week, but book outside that window and you get the atmosphere without the premium pricing.

We evaluated 12 destinations across multiple continents and graded each on what actually matters for February specifically — weather reliability, daily budget, crowd levels, and why this month is better than any other time to visit.

## How We Evaluated

- **Weather reliability** — Average temperatures and precipitation specifically for February. Not "winter" broadly, but February conditions you can plan around.
- **Daily budget** — Per-person costs covering mid-range accommodation, meals, local transport, and one activity per day. Flights excluded since they vary by origin.
- **Crowd level** — Rated 1 (quiet), 2 (busy but manageable), or 3 (peak season crowds). Based on seasonal tourism data.
- **February factor** — What makes this month specifically better than any other time to visit.

## Carnival Destinations

Two cities. Two completely different Carnival experiences. One fact they share: February is when they hit their annual peak.

### 1. Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

**Why February:** Carnival is the single most compelling reason to visit any city in any month on this list. Rio's Carnival 2026 runs February 13-18, with the iconic Sambadrome parades on the 14th and 15th (plus the Champions Parade on the 21st). An estimated 2 million people take to the streets for the blocos (street parties) every day during Carnival week. Nothing else in the travel world comes close to this scale and energy.

**Avg temp:** 25-33°C (77-91°F)
**Daily budget:** $80-180/person
**Crowd level:** 3
**Best for:** Party travelers, culture seekers, music lovers, bucket-list experiences

The Sambadrome experience divides into two tiers. Grandstand tickets in sectors 7-13 run BRL 200-600 ($35-105), offering good views without the VIP markup. Camarote (hospitality box) tickets in the celebrity sectors run BRL 1,500-5,000+ ($265-880) and include open bars and elevated sightlines. Book Sambadrome tickets through the official Liga das Escolas de Samba website — third-party resellers charge 2-3x the face price.

Accommodation in February Rio is expensive by any measure — mid-range hotels in Ipanema or Flamengo run $100-180/night during Carnival week, roughly double the January price. Book 3-4 months in advance or consider staying in Santa Teresa or Botafogo, where boutique guesthouses offer better value and the same access to the blocos.

The blocos are free. That's the secret most first-timers miss. While the Sambadrome parade is the spectacle, the street parties that run daily from February 6 through March 1 are where locals actually celebrate — and they cost nothing to join. Cordão do Bola Preta in Centro draws 2 million people on its Saturday bloco. Monobloco's parade through Ipanema and Leblon is the closest thing to a curated street party experience.

**Insider tip:** Arrive February 10-12 to experience the blocos before the Carnival crowds peak, then stay through February 16-17 for the Champions Parade atmosphere. Flights and hotels get progressively cheaper the further you book from peak Carnival dates.

### 2. Venice, Italy

**Why February:** [Venice](/destinations/venice) Carnival 2026 runs January 31 through February 17. The city's winter low season transforms entirely: elaborate baroque masks fill the streets, the Piazza San Marco hosts daily costume contests, and the iconic Grand Canal becomes a backdrop for one of Europe's most theatrical celebrations. Outside Carnival, February is Venice's emptiest month — hotels drop 50-60% below summer pricing and you can walk across the Rialto Bridge without stopping.

**Avg temp:** 2-8°C (36-46°F)
**Daily budget:** $120-250/person
**Crowd level:** 3 (during Carnival); 1 (pre/post-Carnival)
**Best for:** Culture lovers, photographers, couples, Carnival enthusiasts

Venice Carnival has two modes. The first week (January 31 - February 7) is busy but manageable — hotels are cheaper, the costume crowds are thinner, and you can photograph the masks against empty calli (streets). The final four days (February 14-17) are the peak, with the Flight of the Angel (Volo dell'Angelo) on the 14th seeing 100,000+ spectators in Piazza San Marco.

Hotel pricing reflects this precisely: a mid-range hotel near the Rialto runs EUR 100-150/night in the first week versus EUR 200-350/night for the final weekend. Palazzo apartments on Airbnb follow the same curve. If budget matters, go early in Carnival — you get the same costumes and events for 40% less.

The one unmissable experience: rent or buy a Venetian mask and costume from one of the traditional workshops in Dorsoduro (not the mass-produced plastic versions sold near tourist sites) and participate rather than just observe. Costume rental runs EUR 50-120 for the day. Then photograph yourself on the Accademia Bridge at dawn, before the crowds arrive.

For planning your Venice days beyond Carnival, use [MonkeyTravel's AI trip planner](https://monkeytravel.app) to build a route that covers the off-the-tourist-trail neighborhoods — Cannaregio, Castello, and Sant'Elena — where Venetians actually live.

**Insider tip:** The Carnival grand finale on February 17 features the best fireworks over the lagoon. Watch from the Punta della Dogana or the Zattere waterfront in Dorsoduro — same view as San Marco, without the crush. Get there by 8 PM for a good position.

## Winter Sun

February is peak season for sun seekers fleeing northern winters. These two options cover both short-haul and long-haul needs.

### 3. Tenerife, Canary Islands

**Why February:** Tenerife in February hits its annual sweet spot. The island's position off the West African coast delivers consistent warmth — 18-22°C (64-72°F) — when mainland Europe is at its coldest. February averages 7-8 hours of daily sunshine on the south coast, and rainfall is minimal. It's the easiest warm-weather escape from Europe: a 4-hour flight from most northern cities, no time zone adjustment, and genuinely reliable sun.

**Avg temp:** 18-22°C (64-72°F)
**Daily budget:** $80-150/person
**Crowd level:** 1-2
**Best for:** Sun seekers, families, couples, hikers, budget escape from northern winters

February also brings Tenerife's own Carnival to Santa Cruz de Tenerife — one of the largest in the world after Rio, running through February 22. The Santa Cruz Carnival is free to attend, culturally vibrant, and significantly less chaotic than Rio's. If you're after Carnival atmosphere without intercontinental airfare, this is the answer.

The south coast delivers the most reliable sun: Playa de las Américas, Los Cristianos, and the Golf del Sur area average 7-8 daily sunshine hours even in February. Mid-range apartments near the beach run EUR 40-65/night — still 20% below peak summer pricing. Local restaurants away from the tourist strip serve a €10-12 three-course menú del día including wine.

Mount Teide is the obligatory excursion: cable car to 3,555 meters costs EUR 39, and the views stretch across all seven Canary Islands on clear days. Book in advance — the cable car caps daily visitors.

**Insider tip:** The Teide Observatory runs stargazing tours (EUR 32-45/person) that are genuinely exceptional in February, when clear skies combine with long nights. The altitude eliminates light pollution and makes for some of the best stargazing in Europe. Book a week ahead.

### 4. Dubai, UAE

**Why February:** [Dubai](/destinations/dubai) reaches its weather zenith in February: 17-26°C (63-79°F), essentially zero rainfall, and low humidity. The outdoor spaces that are torture in July — JBR Beach, the Dubai Creek waterfront, the Desert Safari dunes — are genuinely pleasant. February also lands squarely in the Dubai Food Festival and sees the city's golf, tennis, and motorsport calendars firing simultaneously. Valentine's week brings some restaurant surcharges, but hotels actually soften their rates compared to January.

**Avg temp:** 17-26°C (63-79°F)
**Daily budget:** $120-280/person
**Crowd level:** 2-3
**Best for:** Luxury seekers, families, first-time Middle East visitors, outdoor enthusiasts

The Burj Khalifa observation deck at Level 148 (SKY experience) runs $65-80 per person — book 48 hours ahead online to avoid the 40% walk-up premium. The Dubai Desert Conservation Reserve Safari (from $80/person) is worth upgrading from the standard dune-bashing package: smaller groups, a wildlife focus, and overnight options with dinner under the stars.

Budget-conscious February travelers should note that old Dubai (Deira, Bur Dubai) delivers an entirely different experience for a fraction of the marina cost. The Gold Souk, Spice Souk, and Al Seef heritage district are free to explore. An abra (water taxi) crossing of Dubai Creek costs AED 1 ($0.27). Shawarma stands in Deira serve meals for AED 8-12 ($2-3.30).

**Insider tip:** The Miracle Garden (open October-May) is at its February peak — 150 million flowers across 72,000 square meters, featuring structures including a full-size Emirates A380 covered in flowers. Entry is AED 55 ($15). Visit on a weekday morning to beat the weekend crowds.

## Dry Season Destinations

February is peak dry season across tropical Asia and Central America. These conditions are brief — make use of them.

### 5. Thailand

**Why February:** Thailand's dry season peaks in February. In Chiang Mai, January-March brings the clearest skies and most comfortable temperatures of the year — 20-32°C (68-90°F) with low humidity and zero rain. The southern islands (Koh Samui, Koh Phangan, Koh Tao) are in their calm, crystal-clear period on the Gulf of Thailand side. February also brings Chinese New Year celebrations to Bangkok's Yaowarat (Chinatown), which falls on February 17 in 2026 — one of the most atmospheric street celebrations in Southeast Asia.

**Avg temp:** 20-35°C (68-95°F)
**Daily budget:** $40-100/person
**Crowd level:** 2
**Best for:** Budget travelers, divers, culture seekers, foodies, solo travelers

Chiang Mai in February is arguably the most underrated city-in-nature combination in Southeast Asia. The Sunday Walking Street market (Wualai Road) brings handmade crafts and local food at local prices. A full-day cooking class at one of the reputable cooking schools runs $25-40 including a market tour. The temples — Doi Suthep above the city, Wat Chedi Luang in the Old City — are at their atmospheric best in the cooler February air.

The islands operate at peak quality in February. Koh Tao's dive sites see 25+ meter visibility. A PADI Open Water course runs THB 9,500-12,000 ($260-330) — among the cheapest certified courses in the world. Koh Phangan, minus the Full Moon Party crowds of other months, is surprisingly peaceful and beautiful in February.

For a detailed [Bangkok](/destinations/bangkok) itinerary, our [Bangkok 5-day guide](/blog/bangkok-5-day-itinerary) covers the logistics. And our [Bali vs Thailand comparison](/blog/bali-vs-thailand) helps if you're choosing between the two.

**Insider tip:** The Chiang Mai Flower Festival runs the first weekend of February (February 6-8, 2026) — a parade of floats decorated with fresh flowers through the old city moat area. Free to attend and genuinely spectacular. Book accommodation 6-8 weeks ahead as the city fills up for this event.

### 6. Maldives

**Why February:** The Maldives in February is at true peak. The Northeast Monsoon that started in December continues delivering calm seas, excellent visibility (25-40 meters on many dive sites), and daily sunshine averaging 9 hours. Unlike January, the New Year surcharge has fully cleared — February sees hotel and guesthouse rates 20-30% below the January 1-7 peak. Manta ray sightings at cleaning stations in the North Male and Ari Atolls are most consistent February through April.

**Avg temp:** 26-30°C (79-86°F)
**Daily budget:** $150-400/person
**Crowd level:** 3
**Best for:** Divers, snorkelers, honeymooners, Valentine's couples

The guesthouse island model makes the Maldives genuinely accessible. Maafushi, Thulusdhoo, and Fulidhoo all have well-reviewed guesthouses at $60-100/night with beach access, in-house dive operators, and day-trip boats to uninhabited sandbanks. The all-in resort experience (starting at $400+/night at mid-range properties, $800-2,000+ at luxury overwater bungalows) is extraordinary but not the only way to experience the archipelago.

For divers, February brings the best conditions of the year for hammerhead sightings at Rasdhoo Atoll and whale shark encounters in the South Ari Marine Protected Area. A two-tank dive runs $60-90; liveaboard dive packages for 7 nights run $1,200-2,500 all-inclusive.

Valentine's Week (February 9-16) sees resort packages at a 25-40% premium. Book outside this window — February 17-28 offers identical weather with significantly more availability and lower prices.

**Insider tip:** The uninhabited sandbanks (locally called "picnic islands") are a Maldives experience you can access for $30-50 from most guesthouse islands. A half-day trip typically includes snorkeling equipment, lunch, and 3-4 hours on a strip of white sand with no other structures in sight. It's the postcard experience, accessible without the resort price tag.

## Snow & Winter Experiences

### 7. Sapporo, Japan — Snow Festival

**Why February:** The Sapporo Snow Festival (Yuki Matsuri) runs February 4-11, 2026, transforming the city into an open-air sculpture park. Over 200 snow and ice statues — some reaching 25 meters in height — fill three venues: Odori Park (the main site), Susukino (the entertainment district), and the Tsudome outdoor arena. Two million visitors attend over eight days, but the scale of the city means it never feels unmanageable in the way Rio's Carnival does.

**Avg temp:** -6 to -1°C (21-30°F)
**Daily budget:** $100-200/person
**Crowd level:** 2-3
**Best for:** Culture lovers, food travelers, photographers, Japan first-timers looking for something beyond Tokyo

Sapporo's food scene is legitimately extraordinary: Hokkaido produces Japan's finest dairy, seafood, and produce. A bowl of miso ramen at a Susukino restaurant costs JPY 1,000-1,500 ($7-10). The Sapporo Beer Museum offers free entry and beer hall access from JPY 800 ($5.50). Kani Honke on Ekimae-dori is the definitive Hokkaido crab restaurant — dinner for two with multiple crab preparations runs JPY 15,000-25,000 ($100-170).

Outside the Snow Festival window, February Hokkaido offers world-class skiing at Niseko, Rusutsu, and Furano — all within 90 minutes of Sapporo. A JR pass covering Sapporo-Otaru-Furano runs JPY 8,000-12,000 ($55-82) for a 5-day regional ticket.

For broader [Japan](/destinations/tokyo) planning, our [Tokyo 4-day itinerary](/blog/tokyo-4-day-itinerary) and [first trip to Japan guide](/blog/first-trip-to-japan-what-you-need-to-know) cover the essentials.

**Insider tip:** The Odori Park snow sculptures are illuminated after dark (6-10 PM daily), and this is when they're most photogenic — blue-white light against the night sky, no direct sunlight washing out the details. The Susukino Ice World runs simultaneously and is less crowded than the main Odori venue. Night temperatures drop to -10°C (14°F), so dress in proper layers.

### 8. Lapland, Finland — Northern Lights

**Why February:** February is the peak of the Northern Lights season in Finnish Lapland. The long nights (still 16-18 hours of darkness) combined with increasing solar activity in the current 11-year cycle make February one of the statistically best months for aurora sightings. Unlike December and January, February days are noticeably longer — enough daylight for husky sledding, reindeer safaris, and snowshoeing — while nights remain dark enough for reliable aurora viewing.

**Avg temp:** -18 to -8°C (0-18°F)
**Daily budget:** $180-320/person
**Crowd level:** 1-2
**Best for:** Northern Lights chasers, adventure travelers, couples, photographers

Aurora probability in Finnish Lapland in February: approximately 70-80% of clear nights produce visible displays. The trick is "clear nights" — cloud cover is the main variable. Staying 5-7 nights dramatically increases your odds versus a 3-night trip. Base yourself in Inari or Muonio rather than Rovaniemi, which sits further south and has more light pollution.

Guided Northern Lights excursions run EUR 80-160 per person and include transport to dark zones, warm gear rental, and hot drinks. Glass igloos (aurora viewing cabins) at properties like Kakslauttanen or Saariselka Wilderness Hotel run EUR 250-500/night — expensive, but the experience of watching the aurora from your bed is genuinely unmatched.

For more on this, our guide to the [best places to see the Northern Lights](/blog/best-places-to-see-northern-lights) covers the full European and global comparison.

**Insider tip:** Download the Space Weather app and set up real-time Kp index alerts before your trip. A Kp of 4+ means aurora visible even in light-polluted areas; a Kp of 6+ means a full-sky display. Apps allow you to be woken at 2 AM when the show starts — far more reliable than guided tours that run on fixed schedules.

## Summer in the Southern Hemisphere

### 9. New Zealand — South Island

**Why February:** New Zealand's South Island summer peaks in January and February, with February actually the drier of the two months in most regions. The Milford Sound, Queenstown, and the Otago Peninsula are at their most accessible. Temperatures in Queenstown average 16-24°C (61-75°F) — ideal for hiking without the scorching heat of Australia at this time. February also brings the Marlborough Wine Festival (first weekend), the Hokitika Wildfoods Festival, and uncrowded Great Walks compared to Christmas-January bookings.

**Avg temp:** 16-24°C (61-75°F)
**Daily budget:** $120-220/person
**Crowd level:** 1-2
**Best for:** Hikers, adventure travelers, wine lovers, nature photographers, couples

The Great Walks in February require booking but are available — unlike January, when some tracks (Milford, Routeburn) are booked out 6 months ahead. February sees the peak wildlife season on the Otago Peninsula: yellow-eyed penguins, royal albatrosses, and fur seals are all active. A guided wildlife tour from Dunedin costs NZD 70-120 ($42-72).

Queenstown adventure pricing: Bungy at the Kawarau Gorge (the original, 43 meters) costs NZD 245 ($148). Skydiving over Queenstown from 15,000 feet runs NZD 299-349 ($180-210). Jet boating on the Dart River costs NZD 150-200 ($90-120). For non-adrenaline seekers, the Queenstown Gardens and Frankton Arm foreshore walk are free.

Marlborough wine region: the Marlborough Wine Festival (February 7-8, 2026) features 40+ wineries and costs NZD 95 ($57) for a session ticket. Outside festival weekend, cellar door tastings at Cloudy Bay, Wither Hills, and Dog Point Vineyard run NZD 5-20 ($3-12) per person.

**Insider tip:** Rent a campervan from Christchurch rather than Queenstown — it's a 4-hour drive to Queenstown but you pass the spectacular Mackenzie Basin, Lake Tekapo, and Aoraki/Mount Cook along the way. February availability is much better than January and rates drop NZD 15-30/day compared to peak Christmas period.

### 10. Patagonia — Chile and Argentina

**Why February:** February is the single best month to hike Torres del Paine National Park in Chilean Patagonia and the Fitz Roy sector of Los Glaciares in Argentine Patagonia. January is technically summer too, but February brings more stable weather, fewer extreme wind events, and the autumn light that photographers obsess over. The park's W Trek and the classic Fitz Roy lookout trail from El Chaltén are both at their most rewarding in the second half of the month.

**Avg temp:** 6-18°C (43-64°F)
**Daily budget:** $100-200/person
**Crowd level:** 2
**Best for:** Serious hikers, photographers, adventure travelers, wildlife spotters

Torres del Paine: the W Trek takes 5 days and connects the main viewpoints — Mirador Las Torres, the Valle del Francés, and Lago Grey glacier. Refugio accommodation runs $60-120/person/night including dinner and breakfast. Camping costs $10-20/person at designated sites. The CONAF park entrance fee is CLP 21,000 ($23) per person, paid in Chilean pesos at the park entrance.

El Chaltén (Argentina) is a smaller but compelling alternative: the Fitz Roy Base Camp trail is free (no entrance fee to Los Glaciares North), 24 km return, and offers views of one of the world's most photographed granite formations. Accommodation in El Chaltén runs $30-60/night for a dorm in a trekking hostel, $80-150 for a private room.

February wildlife: Magellanic penguins are active at Otway Sound colony (1.5 hours from Punta Arenas, CLP 15,000/$16 entry), and condors soar daily over the Fitz Roy massif. Guanacos are common throughout both parks.

**Insider tip:** Book refugio accommodation in Torres del Paine 4-5 months ahead for February — the trail fills fast. If the W Trek is fully booked, the Mirador Las Torres day hike (return from the Las Torres Central Refugio, 18 km, 800m elevation gain) delivers the park's signature view without multi-day booking requirements. The 4:30 AM start puts you at the mirador at sunrise with 90% fewer people than the midday crowds.

## Cultural & Historical

### 11. Egypt

**Why February:** Egypt in February is close to perfect. Temperatures in Luxor and Aswan range from 10-22°C (50-72°F) — warm enough for outdoor sightseeing, cool enough to actually enjoy standing in an exposed temple for three hours. The Nile is calm, the desert tours are comfortable, and tourist volumes are at their annual low. February avoids both the October-November peak and the summer extremes. Cairo in February sees 12-14°C (54-57°F) — bring a light jacket for evenings.

**Avg temp:** 10-22°C (50-72°F)
**Daily budget:** $50-120/person
**Crowd level:** 1-2
**Best for:** History lovers, budget travelers, photographers, first-time Egypt visitors

The value proposition in February Egypt is extraordinary. A Nile cruise from Luxor to Aswan (4 nights/5 days) runs $250-500 per person all-inclusive at reputable operators — covering accommodation, all meals, guided temple visits, and transport. A guided private day tour to the Valley of the Kings, Karnak, and Luxor Temple costs $40-70 per person from Luxor.

Cairo's big three are genuinely world-class: the Grand Egyptian Museum (opened in its full capacity in 2024) houses over 100,000 artifacts including the complete Tutankhamun collection. Entry runs EGP 450 ($9) for foreigners. The Pyramids of Giza entry is EGP 480 ($10) — the optional interior access to the Great Pyramid costs an additional EGP 600 ($12) and is worth it despite the confined passage.

February tip: book a sunset camel or horse ride around the Pyramids for EGP 300-500 ($6-10) — it's a classic, and the February light at golden hour is extraordinary.

**Insider tip:** Stay in a West Bank hotel in Luxor (across the Nile from the main tourist strip) and you're within walking or cycling distance of the Valley of the Kings, Hatshepsut Temple, and the Colossi of Memnon. Prices run 30-40% lower than the East Bank hotels, and the views across the Nile toward Luxor Temple are better. Rent a bicycle from your hotel for $3-5/day.

### 12. Costa Rica — Pacific Coast

**Why February:** Costa Rica's dry season (December through April) hits its stride in February. The Pacific coast — Guanacaste, the Nicoya Peninsula, and Manuel Antonio — sees day after day of sunshine with 25-32°C (77-90°F) temperatures and minimal rainfall. It's peak season for wildlife: humpback whales, sea turtles, scarlet macaws, and howler monkeys are all active. The national parks are accessible without the mud and trail closures of the rainy season.

**Avg temp:** 22-32°C (72-90°F)
**Daily budget:** $70-150/person
**Crowd level:** 2
**Best for:** Wildlife lovers, families, surfers, eco-travelers, nature photographers

Costa Rica isn't the cheapest destination in Central America, but the wildlife density is unmatched. Manuel Antonio National Park (entry $20/person, open 7 AM-4 PM, closed Tuesdays) is one of the world's most accessible wildlife experiences: white-faced capuchins, sloths, red-eyed tree frogs, and squirrel monkeys are routinely visible on the trails. A licensed guide (mandatory for some park areas, strongly recommended for all) costs $25-40 for a 3-hour walk.

Surf conditions: Tamarindo and Santa Teresa deliver consistent February swells for intermediate surfers. A 2-hour lesson with board and equipment runs $35-50. Nosara is the premium surf destination — smaller, quieter, and with more consistent waves, but accommodation runs 20-30% higher.

Accommodation ranges widely: budget hostels in Tamarindo from $20-30/night (dorm), mid-range boutique hotels in Manuel Antonio from $80-150/night. Package deals combining accommodation and tours from San José run $600-900/person for 7 nights including transfers — often the best value for first-time Costa Rica visitors.

**Insider tip:** The Nicoya Peninsula is Costa Rica's value sweet spot in February. Towns like Montezuma, Malpais, and Sámara are far less developed than Tamarindo, with better beaches, calmer waters for swimming, and accommodation 30-40% cheaper. The Tambor ferry crossing from Puntarenas (90 minutes, $2) gets you there without backtracking through San José.

## How to Pick the Right February Destination

Still deciding? Use these filters:

**If you want a bucket-list experience:** Rio de Janeiro Carnival is once-in-a-lifetime. Nothing else on this list comes close to its scale and energy. Go now — not "someday." The Sapporo Snow Festival is a more manageable version of that same "nowhere else in the world" feeling.

**If budget is the priority:** Egypt ($50-120/day), Thailand ($40-100/day), and Costa Rica ($70-150/day) deliver the most experience per dollar in February. Egypt specifically is extraordinary value for the level of historical significance on offer.

**If weather matters most:** Dubai, Maldives, and Thailand deliver the most reliable sunshine and warmth. Tenerife is the best option for European travelers wanting to minimize flight time.

**If you want nature over crowds:** Patagonia and New Zealand's South Island offer world-class wilderness with February's relatively thin crowds. Both reward at least 7-10 days.

**If it's for Valentine's Day:** Venice Carnival provides the most romantic backdrop on the list. Book the first week of Carnival (before February 14) for the atmosphere without the Valentine's premium.

## Tips for February Travel

1. **Book Carnival destinations immediately.** Rio and Venice fill up 3-5 months ahead. For Rio Carnival 2026, accommodation in February is already pricing at peak levels. Every week of delay costs money.

2. **Valentine's Day surcharge is real but avoidable.** Hotels and restaurants in romantic destinations (Venice, Maldives, Dubai) charge 20-40% premiums for February 14. Book a trip that covers February 8-13 or February 16-21 and pay standard rates for the same experience.

3. **February is the cheapest month to fly in most markets.** US, European, and Australian airlines consistently post their lowest fares in February outside Carnival/Valentine's windows. Set fare alerts in December for February flexibility.

4. **Southern Hemisphere summer is at its peak.** New Zealand, Patagonia, and South Africa (not on this list but worth knowing) are all in peak summer conditions. Book Great Walks accommodation 4-6 months ahead.

5. **Northern Lights season is at its most reliable.** February's long nights plus the current solar maximum (2025-2026) make this one of the best years in a decade for aurora sightings. Lapland accommodations with aurora alert systems are worth the premium.

## FAQ

### What is the best place to visit in February for warm weather?

The most reliable warm-weather destinations in February are the Maldives (26-30°C), Thailand (20-35°C), Dubai (17-26°C), and Tenerife (18-22°C). For travelers in North America, Costa Rica and Cartagena (Colombia) offer excellent Caribbean warmth without the trans-Atlantic flight. For pure reliability with minimal rainfall, the Maldives and the Thai Gulf islands lead the list.

### Is February a good time to visit Europe?

February in mainland Europe is cold and grey in most cities. However, specific European destinations are excellent: Venice Carnival is one of the world's great travel events, Tenerife delivers reliable sunshine, and the Alps offer peak ski conditions. If you're set on a city break, consider Seville or Lisbon — both have milder February weather than northern Europe and are uncrowded.

### How much does Rio Carnival cost?

The main costs for Rio Carnival 2026 are flights, accommodation, and Sambadrome tickets. Mid-range accommodation in Flamengo or Botafogo runs $80-140/night during Carnival week. Sambadrome grandstand tickets range from BRL 200-600 ($35-105) per night; VIP camarote tickets run BRL 1,500-5,000+ ($265-880). Street blocos are entirely free. A realistic total budget for 7 nights in Rio during Carnival (flights excluded) is $800-1,500/person for a mid-range experience.

### Where is the best place to see the Northern Lights in February?

Finnish Lapland (Inari, Muonio, Saariselka) and Norwegian Lapland (Tromsø, Alta) offer the highest probability of Northern Lights sightings in February. The Finnish locations sit further from urban light pollution and generally offer clearer skies. Aurora probability on clear February nights in Finnish Lapland runs 70-80%. February is statistically better than December (less darkness) or January (comparable, but accommodation is slightly cheaper in February at non-peak weeks).

### Can you do Carnival and a beach trip in the same February visit to Brazil?

Yes, and this is actually the optimal Brazil itinerary for February. Rio Carnival runs February 13-18 — spend 5-6 days in Rio for Carnival, then take a 3-hour bus or 1-hour flight to Búzios or Paraty for 4-5 days of beach time. Both are within easy distance of Rio and offer excellent late-summer beach conditions in February. Búzios is upscale and yacht-heavy; Paraty balances colonial architecture, beaches, and jungle waterfalls.
