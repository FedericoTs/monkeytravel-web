# Cheapest Flights 2026: When and Where to Book for the Best Deals

The average domestic round-trip airfare in the US hovers around $340. But dozens of routes consistently clear for under $100 — and some of the best international deals land below $200 round-trip. The difference between paying $340 and paying $94 isn't luck. It's timing, route selection, and a few specific tactics most travelers skip.

Here's what the data actually shows for 2026.

---

## The 10 Cheapest Domestic Routes in the US

These aren't one-off flash sales. These are routes with structurally low average prices due to competition between carriers, airport capacity, or secondary hub dynamics.

| Route | Avg Round-Trip | Notes |
|-------|---------------|-------|
| Chicago (MDW/ORD) | **$94** | Southwest's MDW focus drives prices down year-round |
| Fort Lauderdale (FLL) | **$95** | Ultra-low-cost carrier hub; Spirit, Frontier compete hard |
| Pittsburgh (PIT) | **$104** | Underserved market keeps fares low; easy to find sub-$90 sales |
| Dallas (DAL/DFW) | **$108** | Two major airports create fierce competition |
| Baltimore (BWI) | **$112** | Southwest hub effect; often cheaper than nearby DC airports |
| Las Vegas (LAS) | **$118** | High flight frequency, hotel-bundled pricing keeps fares suppressed |
| Phoenix (PHX) | **$122** | Sun Belt growth market; multiple low-cost carriers compete |
| Denver (DEN) | **$127** | Frontier's home hub; frequent seat sales |
| Orlando (MCO) | **$131** | High volume = high competition; package deals lower effective fares |
| Tampa (TPA) | **$134** | Emerging low-cost hub; growth corridor from Northeast and Midwest |

The pattern: secondary airports within multi-airport metros (MDW vs. ORD, DAL vs. DFW, FLL vs. MIA, BWI vs. DCA/IAD) consistently produce lower fares. If you're flexible on which airport you use, this alone can save $50-100 on a round-trip.

## The Booking Calendar: When to Book by Season

Timing your purchase matters as much as which route you choose. These windows reflect when fares are typically lowest relative to departure, based on airline pricing patterns and historical booking data.

### Domestic US Flights

| Season | Departure | Book This Window | Expected Savings |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Spring (Mar-May) | March-May | 4-6 weeks out | 10-15% vs. peak |
| Summer (Jun-Aug) | June-August | **2-3 months ahead** | 15-25% vs. last-minute |
| Fall (Sep-Nov) | September-November | 3-5 weeks out | 10-20% |
| Winter holidays | Dec 20-Jan 5 | **4-5 months ahead** | 20-30% vs. late booking |

Summer is where the booking window matters most. Domestic summer flights booked 8-10 weeks before departure average 18% cheaper than the same routes booked 2 weeks out. The sweet spot is roughly June and July departures booked in April.

### International Flights

| Season | Departure | Book This Window | Expected Savings |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Spring (Mar-May) | March-May | 3-4 months ahead | 15-20% |
| Summer (Jun-Aug) | June-August | **4-6 months ahead** | 20-30% vs. late booking |
| Fall (Sep-Nov) | September-November | 6-8 weeks out | 10-20% |
| Winter (Dec-Feb) | December-February | 4-6 months ahead | 25-35% on peak dates |

International summer requires more lead time — 4-6 months is the accepted range, with European destinations like Madrid and Rome requiring the earlier end of that window. If you're planning a June trip to Europe, March is your target booking window.

## The Cheapest Days and Months to Fly

### Days of the Week

Wednesday is consistently the cheapest day to fly, with average fares running up to 20% lower than weekend departures. The hierarchy looks like this:

- **Wednesday**: Cheapest — lighter business and leisure demand
- **Tuesday / Saturday**: Second-cheapest tier
- **Thursday**: Mid-range
- **Monday / Friday**: Premium days (business travel surge)
- **Sunday**: Often the most expensive domestic day due to leisure return traffic

If you can shift a Friday departure to Wednesday, the savings are real. On a $300 fare, 20% is $60 per person — $120 for two people, which covers two nights of accommodation in many destinations.

### Cheapest Months to Fly Domestically

January and February are the cheapest months for domestic US travel — the post-holiday hangover kills demand, and airlines discount aggressively to fill seats. September and October are the second-best window, after summer rush and before Thanksgiving.

The most expensive periods: Thanksgiving week, Christmas through New Year's, and the peak of summer (last two weeks of July through mid-August). Avoid these if price is a priority, or book 4-5 months ahead to lock in before yield management kicks in.

### Cheapest Month for Key International Destinations

| Destination | Cheapest Month | Most Expensive Month |

The pattern is consistent: shoulder seasons (spring and fall, excluding holiday weeks) are where the deals live. The cheapest international month is almost always 1-2 months before or after the main tourist surge, not the off-season low-traffic winter window that most people assume.

## Flight Booking Strategies That Actually Work

### 1. Flexible Dates Search

The single most effective tactic, used by the fewest people. Every major flight search tool — Google Flights, Kayak, Skyscanner — has a calendar view or flexible dates matrix. Pull it up before you have fixed dates. You'll regularly see $40-80 swings between adjacent days on the same route. That difference is $80-160 for a round-trip on one route, zero effort.

Google Flights' "Price Graph" shows fare variation across an entire month at a glance. If your dates are movable, spend five minutes here before booking anything.

### 2. Error Fares

Airlines and booking systems make pricing mistakes — a missing digit, a currency conversion error, a domestic leg accidentally priced as a segment of a long-haul itinerary. These fares are real, bookable, and usually honored by the airline (especially if you receive a confirmation email). They're gone within hours.

How to catch them: sign up for dedicated error fare services. Scott's Cheap Flights (now Going), Secret Flying, and Airfarewatchdog all alert subscribers to verified mistake fares. The free tier on Going is sufficient to catch 3-5 legitimate error fares per year. The paid tier sees significantly more, including business class mistake fares to Europe and Asia for $200-400 round-trip.

### 3. Hidden City Ticketing

This tactic is controversial but legal. It works like this: a nonstop flight from New York to Denver might cost $250, but a connecting itinerary from New York to Las Vegas with a layover in Denver costs $160. You book the Las Vegas ticket, get off in Denver, and skip the second leg.

The risks are real: you can only do this with carry-on luggage (checked bags go to the final destination), airlines can theoretically ban frequent-flier accounts for systematic use, and you must book one-way (not round-trip, because the airline would cancel your return leg). Use sparingly and understand what you're doing before booking.

Skiplagged.com specifically identifies these hidden city routes and shows the fare difference.

### 4. Use Incognito Mode (It's Partially True)

Cookies do track your search behavior, and some booking sites do show higher prices to repeat searchers. The effect is smaller than the internet claims, but it's not zero. Use a private/incognito browser for flight searches and clear cookies when comparing fares across sessions.

### 5. Set Price Alerts, Then Actually Use Them

Google Flights and Kayak both let you track specific routes and notify you when prices drop. The key is tracking a route 4-8 weeks before it enters your ideal booking window — not the day before you want to book. Set an alert in January for a summer flight. When prices drop to your target, you'll know within hours.

### 6. Consider Positioning Flights

If you're near a secondary airport with cheap fares to international hubs (Fort Lauderdale, Baltimore, Newark), a $60-80 positioning flight can save you $200+ on the transatlantic leg. Flying FLL to Madrid instead of JFK to Madrid is frequently $100-150 cheaper on the main leg, even after adding the positioning flight cost.

## Budget Tactics That Compound the Flight Savings

Getting a cheap flight is step one. Keeping costs low on the ground is where the real savings accumulate.

**Fly into secondary cities, then use trains.** Flying into Bucharest (cheaper) and taking a 2.5-hour train to Brasov costs roughly the same as flying directly into a Brasov-adjacent airport — except there isn't one. This applies across Europe: Budapest instead of Vienna, Porto instead of Lisbon, Bologna instead of Florence.

**Eat where locals eat.** This is not a revolutionary insight, but the execution matters. Lunch menus (fixed-price midday meals) in Spain, Portugal, and France are typically 40-60% cheaper than dinner for equivalent quality. In Southeast Asia, street food stalls adjacent to markets — not the tourist-facing ones on main walking streets — are where the real prices are.

**Use public transit from airports.** The taxi from El Salvador's airport to San Salvador center is $25-30. The bus is $1. In Bangkok, the Airport Rail Link to the city center is $3. In Madrid, the metro from Barajas is $5 — the taxi is $35+. This is not a hardship calculation; airport rail is often faster than taxis in traffic.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the cheapest day to book flights in 2026?

Wednesday is consistently the cheapest day to fly, with fares averaging up to 20% lower than weekend departures. Tuesday and Saturday are the next-cheapest tier. For booking (as opposed to flying), Tuesday afternoons have historically seen airlines post sales and competitors match them — but the day you book matters far less than how far in advance you book.

### How far in advance should I book a cheap flight?

For domestic US flights in summer, book 2-3 months ahead. For international summer travel, 4-6 months is the target window. Outside of summer and holiday periods, 3-6 weeks before departure is often sufficient for domestic routes and 6-10 weeks for international. Last-minute fares (within 2 weeks) are almost never cheaper except on specific business routes where airlines discount unsold seats.

### Is Fort Lauderdale really cheaper than Miami airport?

Yes, consistently. FLL is a hub for Spirit and Frontier, the two most aggressive low-cost carriers in the US. Fares out of FLL to common routes average 15-25% less than MIA. If you're in South Florida or traveling from a destination with direct service to FLL, it's worth checking the fare difference.

### What's an error fare and how do I find one?

Error fares are airline pricing mistakes — incorrectly entered fares that appear on booking systems at a fraction of the intended price. They're real, they're bookable, and most airlines honor them once a confirmation email is issued. They disappear within hours. The best way to find them is through Going (formerly Scott's Cheap Flights), Secret Flying, or Airfarewatchdog. Free newsletter tiers are sufficient for most travelers.

### Does hidden city ticketing actually work?

Yes, but with important caveats. You must travel with carry-on only (checked bags go to the final destination), book one-way trips (not round-trips), and understand that airlines technically prohibit it in their terms. It's legal but against airline rules. Skiplagged.com is the main tool for finding these routes. Use it occasionally, not systematically, and don't use it if you have elite status you want to protect.
