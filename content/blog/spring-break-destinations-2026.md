# Spring Break Destinations 2026: 12 Trending Picks (Google Data)

Spring break has changed. According to Google Flights data, travelers in 2026 are abandoning the traditional party-hub playbook — Cancún boozy all-inclusives, Daytona Beach crowds, Miami South Beach gridlock — in favor of waterfalls, volcanoes, mountain towns, and cities with actual culture. The share of Americans actively planning spring travel jumped from 48% to 61% year-over-year. They just want to go somewhere different.

The data backs this up with some striking numbers: Albuquerque searches up 204%, Columbus up 184%, Omaha up 182%, Hong Kong up 103%. These are not beach party destinations. This is a generation of travelers using spring break to do something they'll actually remember.

We pulled the Google Flights trending data, cross-referenced it with realistic daily costs and March-April weather windows, and built a list of 12 destinations that are worth the trip — not just trending on a spreadsheet. Six domestic US picks, six international. No filler.

## 6 Trending Domestic Destinations

### 1. Hilo, Hawaii — The #1 Trending US Destination

**Why trending:** Hilo holds the #1 spot in Google's spring break trending data for 2026, and it makes sense. While Maui and Honolulu fight over the same crowds, the Big Island's wet side is finally getting recognition for what it actually offers: active lava flows at Hawaii Volcanoes National Park, 80-foot waterfalls at Akaka Falls, black sand beaches with no resort infrastructure, and a farmers market that's been running every Wednesday and Saturday since 1988. It does not have a Cheesecake Factory. That's the point.

**Daily budget:** $120–180/person (mid-range hotel, meals, one activity)
**March-April weather:** 75–82°F (24–28°C), frequent light rain on the east side — bring a rain jacket, it clears fast
**What to do:** Lava viewing at Hawaii Volcanoes NP, Akaka Falls hike, snorkeling at Two Step (Honaunau Bay), Rainbow Falls at sunrise, Hilo Farmers Market

**Insider tip:** Book a lava boat tour at night rather than the chain-of-craters overlook hike. Watching molten rock hit the ocean in darkness is a legitimate once-in-a-lifetime experience, costs around $90–120, and most people don't know it exists.

### 2. Sarasota, Florida — The Florida West Coast Secret

**Why trending:** Five of Google's top 10 trending spring break spots are on Florida's west coast, and Sarasota is the standout. It has everything the east coast resorts promise — white sand, warm Gulf water, seafood — without the spring break chaos. Siesta Key Beach has consistently ranked as the #1 beach in the US by Dr. Beach, and the water temperature in March is already around 70°F (21°C). The Ringling Museum, the Sarasota Opera, and an actual arts scene round out what's on offer.

**Daily budget:** $110–170/person
**March-April weather:** 75–83°F (24–28°C), low humidity, mostly sunny — the best weather window of the Florida year
**What to do:** Siesta Key Beach, Mote Marine Aquarium, Ringling Museum of Art, kayaking through Myakka River State Park, sunset at Point of Rocks

**Insider tip:** Siesta Key Village is walkable from the beach and has good casual restaurants at real prices. Skip the oceanfront resort dining — it's all the same $22 fish tacos — and eat at Fandango's or Lickety Split for food locals actually choose.

### 3. Asheville, North Carolina — The Mountain Alternative

**Why trending:** Every beach on the East Coast is accounted for. Asheville is the answer for travelers who want spring break without sand in their shoes. The Blue Ridge Mountains in late March and early April are emerging from winter into wildflower season — trilliums, serviceberries, and eventually the famous rhododendrons start blooming. The city itself has more breweries per capita than almost anywhere in the US, a serious food scene, and the Biltmore Estate (America's largest private home) as a legitimate anchor attraction.

**Daily budget:** $100–150/person
**March-April weather:** 55–68°F (13–20°C) — cool, layering weather, with occasional rain. Not beach weather. That's the point.
**What to do:** Blue Ridge Parkway scenic drives, hiking at Black Balsam Knob or Max Patch, downtown Asheville breweries and galleries, Biltmore Estate, whitewater rafting on the Nantahala

**Insider tip:** The Rat, a small bar on Lexington Avenue, has live music most nights for free or a $5 cover. It feels like discovering a neighborhood bar in Austin before Austin became Austin. Asheville's live music scene is genuinely underrated.

### 4. Las Vegas, Nevada — The Perennial That Earns Its Spot

**Why trending:** Las Vegas doesn't need to trend — it just keeps showing up because it works. March and April are legitimately the best months to visit: temperatures hit 70–80°F (21–27°C) without the brutal summer heat that makes outdoor pools feel like hot tubs. No spring break stigma here, either — Las Vegas attracts everyone from bachelor parties to retirees to families who know about the free attractions (Bellagio fountains, Fremont Street, natural history museum). The new Sphere concert venue continues to draw people who'd never otherwise make the trip.

**Daily budget:** $130–220/person (ranges wildly based on gambling)
**March-April weather:** 68–80°F (20–27°C), almost zero rain, sunny every day
**What to do:** Sphere experience, Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area (30 min drive), Seven Magic Mountains art installation, Fremont Street, day trip to Valley of Fire State Park

**Insider tip:** Valley of Fire State Park is 55 miles from the Strip, costs $15 to enter, and has Martian-red sandstone formations that are more dramatic than anything on the Strip. Most Vegas visitors never leave the city. That's their loss.

### 5. Key West, Florida — The End of the Road

**Why trending:** Key West is the most culturally distinct place in Florida and one of the most distinctive in the US. The 113-mile drive down the Overseas Highway through 42 bridges over open ocean is itself the destination. In spring, the water reaches 77°F (25°C), the wind is low, and snorkeling on the coral reef is at its clearest visibility of the year. The town itself runs on a schedule that ignores the rest of the country — sunset at Mallory Square is a daily community event, not a tourist gimmick.

**Daily budget:** $140–200/person
**March-April weather:** 77–83°F (25–28°C), light breezes, minimal rain
**What to do:** Snorkeling at Looe Key Reef, kayaking through mangrove tunnels, Hemingway Home and Museum, Dry Tortugas National Park day trip, sunset at Mallory Square

**Insider tip:** The Dry Tortugas — a remote national park 70 miles west of Key West accessible only by ferry or seaplane — has the most pristine coral reefs in the US and a 19th-century brick fort. The ferry costs around $200 round-trip and books up weeks in advance. Worth every dollar.

### 6. Albuquerque, New Mexico — Up 204%

**Why trending:** A 204% search increase is hard to explain with a single reason. Part of it is the film and TV tourism effect — Breaking Bad and Better Call Saul brought Albuquerque into pop culture in a lasting way. Part of it is the food: New Mexico green and red chili cuisine is unlike anything else in the American food landscape, and Albuquerque is the best place to eat it. Part of it is the landscape — the Rio Grande bosque, the Sandia Mountains turning pink at sunset, and Petroglyph National Monument on the western edge of the city. Spring is also balloon season, with smaller events preceding the famous October International Balloon Fiesta.

**Daily budget:** $80–120/person (one of the most affordable destinations on this list)
**March-April weather:** 58–72°F (14–22°C), low humidity, very little rain, sunny most days
**What to do:** Old Town Albuquerque, Sandia Peak Tramway (longest aerial tramway in North America), Petroglyph National Monument, Meow Wolf (Albuquerque location), Rio Grande Nature Center

**Insider tip:** The Duran's Central Pharmacy lunch counter on Central Avenue has served New Mexican food since 1956 and remains one of the best meals in the state. The green chili cheeseburger costs $8. Order the Christmas style (red and green chili both) on anything.

## 6 Trending International Destinations

### 7. Cancún, Mexico — #1 International in 13 States

**Why trending:** Cancún is the most searched international spring break destination in 13 US states for a reason: it works at every budget level. The Hotel Zone all-inclusives are genuinely cost-effective for families and groups. But Cancún in 2026 is also increasingly a base for travelers who want to see the Yucatán Peninsula — cenotes, Chichen Itza, Tulum's archaeological zone (separate from the overbuilt beach town), and the Sian Ka'an Biosphere Reserve. The airport is the easiest entry point for Mexico's most spectacular region.

**Daily budget:** $70–130/person (all-inclusive significantly changes this equation)
**March-April weather:** 82–88°F (28–31°C), low rain, warm Caribbean water around 80°F (27°C)
**What to do:** Cenote Dos Ojos snorkeling, Chichen Itza (arrive at opening — 8 AM), Isla Mujeres day trip, Tulum archaeological zone, kitesurfing at Playa Norte

**Insider tip:** Rent a car for one day and drive 20 minutes south to Puerto Morelos. It's a small fishing village with a coral reef directly offshore, no beach clubs, and seafood restaurants where a full meal with drinks is $15–20 per person. It's what Tulum's beach town was 15 years ago.

### 8. Hong Kong — Up 103%

**Why trending:** A 103% search increase signals something substantive is happening. Hong Kong's international tourism is recovering strongly, and the city is actively offering incentives — including free attractions, tourism vouchers, and new cultural openings. The combination of world-class food (Michelin-starred restaurants are more accessible here than anywhere), dramatic Victoria Harbour views, efficient public transit, and the contrast between skyscrapers and hiking trails makes it one of the genuinely great cities to visit. March and April are the best months: mild, low rain, before summer humidity arrives.

**Daily budget:** $100–160/person
**March-April weather:** 64–75°F (18–24°C), low rain, mild — ideal walking weather
**What to do:** Victoria Peak at dusk, dim sum at Tim Ho Wan, Kowloon's Temple Street Night Market, hiking the Dragon's Back trail, Hong Kong Museum of History, ferry to Lantau Island and Giant Buddha

**Insider tip:** The MTR (subway) covers almost everything and costs $1–3 per journey. The Star Ferry across Victoria Harbour costs HK$3.40 (about $0.44) and is one of the most iconic transit rides in the world. Don't take a taxi when the MTR goes there — it's always faster and costs a fraction of the price.

### 9. Montreal, Canada — Up 83%

**Why trending:** Montreal in spring is a specific experience: a city collectively exhaling after a brutal winter. By late March and April, the terrasses (patios) reappear, the festivals begin ramping up, and the city's famous joie de vivre becomes visible to visitors in a way it isn't in February. The food scene is world-class — BYOB restaurants (bring your own wine), the St. Lawrence Market area, a bagel rivalry between two bakeries that has run for decades. And the Canadian dollar exchange rate makes it significantly cheaper than US cities of comparable quality.

**Daily budget:** $85–130/person
**March-April weather:** 34–52°F (1–11°C) in March, warming to 52–64°F (11–18°C) in April — dress in layers
**What to do:** Old Montreal cobblestone streets, St. Joseph's Oratory (largest church in Canada), Mile End neighborhood, Montreal Museum of Fine Arts, Plateau-Mont-Royal for street art and independent restaurants

**Insider tip:** Au Pied de Cochon is the most celebrated restaurant in Montreal — nose-to-tail Québécois cooking by chef Martin Picard. It's not cheap ($60–80/person) but it's a genuinely unique dining experience you can't replicate anywhere else. Book 3–4 weeks ahead and go hungry.

### 10. Dubai, UAE — Up 44%

**Why trending:** Dubai in March and April is a genuinely different proposition from Dubai in July, when it's 115°F (46°C) and even the outdoor malls feel hostile. Spring brings temperatures in the 80s°F (27–32°C), low humidity, and an outdoor lifestyle that makes the city's scale comprehensible. The Expo City legacy, the new Museum of the Future, and the Dubai Frame have added cultural depth to what used to be purely a luxury-shopping and skyscraper-viewing destination. Direct flights from US hubs have also expanded significantly.

**Daily budget:** $130–200/person (spikes considerably if you stay on the Palm or go to high-end restaurants)
**March-April weather:** 77–91°F (25–33°C), almost zero rain, sunny
**What to do:** Burj Khalifa observation deck (book in advance), Dubai Frame, Museum of the Future, Al Fahidi Historical Neighbourhood (the "old Dubai"), desert safari at sunset, spice and gold souks

**Insider tip:** The Burj Khalifa observation deck at the top ("At the Top Sky" on floor 148) costs around $75–100 and can be crowded. For a similar or better view at a fraction of the cost, have drinks at the Atmosphere Lounge on floor 122 — the minimum spend ($30–40) gets you the view without the queues.

### 11. Lisbon, Portugal — The European Value Leader

**Why trending:** Lisbon in spring is the sweet spot: mild temperatures, lower prices than summer peak, fewer crowds, and all the charm intact. The city sits on seven hills above the Tagus river, operates on a food-and-wine culture that keeps prices honest (a glass of local wine at a neighborhood bar is $2–3), and offers a neighborhood walk — from Alfama's fado-tinged alleys to LX Factory's repurposed industrial market — that rivals any city in Europe. March and April are ideal before summer heat and tourist volume both spike.

**Daily budget:** $75–115/person
**March-April weather:** 57–68°F (14–20°C), some rain in March clearing to mostly dry in April
**What to do:** Belém Tower and Jerónimos Monastery, Alfama neighborhood and São Jorge Castle, LX Factory weekend market, Sintra day trip (45 minutes by train), pastéis de nata at Pastéis de Belém

**Insider tip:** Take the train from Lisbon's Cais do Sodré station to Cascais ($2.50, 40 minutes). The coastal line passes through Estoril and Cascais — a small resort town with a beach, a casino, and restaurants where you can eat a full grilled-fish lunch for $12. It's the Portuguese Riviera, mostly visited by locals.

### 12. Cartagena, Colombia — The Underrated Pick

**Why trending:** Cartagena doesn't appear in Google's top-line trending data, but it earns inclusion here because it represents exactly where spring break travel is headed: a walled colonial city on the Caribbean that combines beach access, a UNESCO World Heritage historic center, extraordinary food, and Latin American energy at prices that make most US destinations feel extravagant. Colombia's safety profile has improved dramatically over the past decade, and Cartagena specifically has been welcoming international tourism for years.

**Daily budget:** $65–110/person
**March-April weather:** 87–92°F (31–33°C), hot, low rain, Caribbean water around 82°F (28°C)
**What to do:** Walking the walled city (Ciudad Amurallada), Playa Blanca day trip, sunset cocktails at Café del Mar on the city walls, Rosario Islands snorkeling, Getsemani neighborhood street food

**Insider tip:** The Getsemani neighborhood — historically avoided in older guidebooks — is now the most interesting part of Cartagena. Street art, local restaurants, rooftop bars, and the Plaza de la Trinidad, where locals gather every evening. Eat at a neighborhood arepas spot for $3–4 rather than the tourist restaurants inside the walled city charging $20 for the same food.

## Planning Your Spring Break Trip

Spring break 2026 planning is up 27% year-over-year, which means the flights and hotels you want will fill up faster than usual. A few practical notes:

**Book flights first.** For US domestic destinations, 6–8 weeks out is the sweet spot. For international (especially Hong Kong, Dubai, Lisbon), 2–3 months ahead. Google Flights price alerts are your best tool — set one for each destination you're considering and wait for a dip.

**March vs. April matters.** March is peak spring break traffic (higher prices, more crowds). April is quieter, cheaper, and often better weather. If your schedule is flexible, April wins almost every time.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the #1 trending spring break destination in 2026 according to Google?

According to Google Flights data, Hilo, Hawaii is the #1 trending domestic spring break destination for 2026. For international destinations, Cancún ranks #1 in 13 US states. The broader trend shows travelers moving toward nature and cultural destinations over traditional party hubs.

### What is the cheapest spring break destination for 2026?

Albuquerque, New Mexico ($80–120/day) and Cartagena, Colombia ($65–110/day) are the most affordable destinations on this list. Domestically, Albuquerque is dramatically cheaper than Hawaii or Florida and significantly underrated. Internationally, Cartagena offers Caribbean weather and culture at prices that undercut almost every competing destination.

### Why are searches for Albuquerque, Columbus, and Omaha up so much for spring break 2026?

The data shows a shift toward domestic cities that offer authentic experiences at lower prices — a reaction to several years of overcrowded, overpriced coastal destinations. Albuquerque (+204%) specifically benefits from cultural tourism tied to the Breaking Bad / Better Call Saul filming locations, plus a food scene (New Mexican chili cuisine) that travelers are increasingly seeking out.

### Is Hong Kong safe to visit for spring break 2026?

Yes. Hong Kong has a very low crime rate and is one of the safest major cities in Asia for tourists. The city has invested significantly in tourism recovery, and March-April is the best weather window — mild, low rain, before summer humidity. The MTR transit system is excellent, English is widely spoken in tourist areas, and the food and cultural offerings are world-class.

### Should I book spring break now or wait for prices to drop?

Book now for popular destinations. Spring travel planning is up 27% year-over-year, which puts real pressure on flight and hotel availability. Hilo, Key West, and Cancún especially will see price increases as March dates fill. For flights, use Google Flights price tracking alerts. For hotels, book refundable rates now and keep watching — if prices drop, re-book.
