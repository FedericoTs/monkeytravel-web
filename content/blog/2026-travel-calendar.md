# The 2026 Travel Calendar: Where to Go Every Month, by Conditions Not Hype

I write this from Bologna, where the May rain has finally let up and the swallows are back over the rooftops. I'm a cultural anthropologist by training, and my job — the part of it that involves writing for MonkeyTravel — is to test the difference between what travel marketing promises about a place and what is actually happening there in a given month. The two are often very far apart.

Most "best time to visit" advice is useless. It averages a country into a single line ("Italy is best in spring or fall") and leaves you to figure out whether that means Sicily in March or the Dolomites in October — destinations with almost nothing in common climatically. It ignores the festival you'd want to plan around, the monsoon that might ruin a beach week 200 km from a perfectly dry one, and the way prices on the same hotel can swing 60% across two adjacent weeks. It treats December in Vienna and December in Cape Town as if they're the same problem.

This is the alternative. One pillar, twelve months, and for each month the two or three destinations that are demonstrably at their peak — not "good," not "fine," but the right place to be in that exact window. Everything below is built on five years of chasing seasonal travel deliberately: northern lights in Tromsø in February 2025, fall foliage along Vermont's Route 100 in October 2024, the Kerala backwaters during the southwest monsoon in July 2023, sakura in Kyoto in early April 2022. I keep a spreadsheet. The spreadsheet has columns for actual sky cover, sea temperature, hotel-rate spike percentages, and how many cruise ships docked the day I walked the city walls. It's not glamorous. It's the only way I know to make these recommendations honest.

A few principles before the calendar starts.

**Picks are conditions-driven, not popularity-driven.** Tokyo is on this calendar four times because the city does four genuinely different things across the year — sakura in early April, hydrangeas and Sanno Matsuri in June, koyo foliage in November, illuminations and seasonal sashimi in December. Bali appears five times because dry season and wet season produce two different islands at very different prices. I trimmed each month from twelve destinations down to two or three because the listicle format hides the trade-offs. Two strong picks are more useful than ten generic ones.

**Every budget range below covers accommodation, food, local transport, and one activity per day — flights excluded since they vary by origin.** Where you see "$80-140/day," that's per person, mid-range. Festival weeks and Christmas/New Year corridors are flagged separately because they break the average.

**I'll tell you when not to go.** Don't go to Bali in February if rain ruins your trip. Don't go to Marrakech in July unless your riad has a plunge pool. Don't book Santorini in August unless you have already accepted the price. The month-by-month "avoid" lines are short but load-bearing.

One more thing. The single most useful question is not "where should I go in March" — it's "what do I want from this trip?" The picking-the-right-month section near the end works backward from goals (warmest beach, lowest cost, biggest event) and is, for most readers, the right place to start.

## January: Shoulder-Season Tropics and Northern Lights at Their Peak

January is the year's reset. Post-holiday airfares drop 20-40% from their December peaks. Half the planet is sitting in either prime dry season or peak winter sports conditions. The mistake people make is defaulting to a generic Caribbean resort. The smarter move is targeting the destinations that January does better than any other month.

### Lapland, Finland — Northern Lights in a Solar Maximum Year

The 2025-2026 season sits near the peak of the 11-year solar cycle, which means aurora displays are statistically more frequent and more intense than they will be again for another decade. Finnish Lapland delivers 65-75% aurora visibility on clear January nights — the highest probability of any month, helped by 18-20 hours of darkness.

- **Avg temp:** -15 to -6°C (5-21°F)
- **Daily budget:** $180-320/person
- **What makes January right:** Polar night peaks, solar activity remains elevated, and aurora-viewing accommodations are at their most theatrical.
- **Insider tip:** Skip Rovaniemi if Northern Lights are your priority — too far south, too much light pollution. Base in Inari or Saariselkä, 300 km north, where Sami culture is more authentic and the dark zones are deeper. Bus from Rovaniemi to Inari runs daily for EUR 40-55. For broader strategy, see our guide to the [best places to see the Northern Lights](/blog/best-places-to-see-northern-lights).

### Bangkok, Thailand — The Reliably Good Time

Bangkok in January is the closest thing to a guaranteed good week in Southeast Asia. Heart of dry season, near-zero rainfall, 22-32°C, day after day of clear skies. Post-New Year holiday spike has cleared, hotel pricing returns to normal, and the city operates at peak efficiency — the Grand Palace and Wat Pho without the April heat or September rain.

- **Avg temp:** 22-32°C (72-90°F)
- **Daily budget:** $40-100/person
- **What makes January right:** The single most reliable weather window of the year for the city, and it dovetails into the Chinese New Year decoration season in Yaowarat by late January.
- **Insider tip:** Chinese New Year falls on February 17 in 2026, but Yaowarat (Chinatown) starts decorating from late January — red lantern displays, special menus, none of the actual holiday crowds yet. For a five-day plan, see our [Bangkok 5-day itinerary](/blog/bangkok-5-day-itinerary).

### Marrakech, Morocco — Best-Value Cultural Destination of Winter

January in [Marrakech](/destinations/marrakech) drops daytime highs to a manageable 18°C (64°F) and tourist volumes to their annual low. Riad prices crater 40-60% versus spring. The Atlas Mountains hold snow on their peaks through January, providing the dramatic backdrop the postcards promise but rarely deliver.

- **Avg temp:** 6-18°C (43-64°F)
- **Daily budget:** $45-110/person
- **What makes January right:** Lowest tourist density of the year combined with the lowest accommodation pricing.
- **Insider tip:** Evenings drop to 6°C. Rooftop terraces are a summer move; in January, book a riad with a fireplace and eat tagine by candlelight. A multi-course dinner runs $12-20 per person.

**Avoid in January:** The Maldives in the first week (New Year surcharge inflates rates 15-25%), and any Caribbean island still recovering from late hurricane season — book the second or third week instead.

## February: Carnival, Solar-Maximum Aurora, and Southern Hemisphere Summer Peak

February is the most underrated month on the calendar. Airfares sit at their annual floor in most markets, the southern hemisphere is at peak summer, and Carnival turns two of the world's great cities into spectacles. Valentine's Day creates a price spike from February 9-16 in romantic destinations — book outside that window for identical conditions at 25-40% lower rates.

### Rio de Janeiro, Brazil — The One Reason People Cross the Atlantic in February

Carnival 2026 runs February 13-18, with the Sambadrome parades on the 14th and 15th and the Champions Parade on the 21st. An estimated 2 million people take to the streets for the blocos every day during Carnival week. Nothing else in travel comes close to this scale.

- **Avg temp:** 25-33°C (77-91°F)
- **Daily budget:** $80-180/person
- **What makes February right:** The blocos are free, the atmosphere is genuinely citywide, and February is also peak Brazilian summer beach weather.
- **Insider tip:** Sambadrome grandstand tickets in sectors 7-13 run BRL 200-600 ($35-105) — book through the official Liga das Escolas de Samba website, never resellers. Arrive February 10-12 for blocos before the Carnival peak, stay through the 17th for the Champions Parade atmosphere. Pair Rio with Paraty or Búzios for 4-5 days of beach decompression after the chaos.

### Egypt — Perfect-Weather Window for the Greatest Concentration of History on Earth

February in Egypt is close to the platonic ideal of sightseeing weather: Luxor and Aswan run 10-22°C (50-72°F), the Nile is calm, the desert is comfortable, and tourist volumes are at their annual low. A 4-night Nile cruise at reputable operators runs $250-500 per person all-inclusive. The Grand Egyptian Museum (opened in full 2024) houses 100,000+ artifacts including the complete Tutankhamun collection. EGP 450 (~$9) for foreigners.

- **Avg temp:** 10-22°C (50-72°F)
- **Daily budget:** $50-120/person
- **What makes February right:** You can stand in an exposed temple courtyard for three hours without becoming a casualty.
- **Insider tip:** Stay on the West Bank in Luxor — across the Nile from the main tourist strip, walking or cycling distance to the Valley of the Kings, 30-40% cheaper than East Bank hotels. Bicycle rental: $3-5/day.

### Lapland (still excellent) and Patagonia (peak trekking)

If January didn't work for the aurora, February in Finnish Lapland is statistically as strong, with 70-80% aurora probability on clear nights and noticeably longer daytime activity windows for husky sledding and reindeer safaris. Patagonia's Torres del Paine W Trek hits its single best weather month — more stable winds than January, the autumn light photographers obsess over. Refugio bookings for February need to happen 4-5 months ahead.

**Avoid in February:** Mainland European city breaks for warmth. Paris and Rome are mild but grey, with shorter daylight than the brochures suggest. Save those for March-April or October. Also avoid Bali if you can't tolerate rain — February sits in the heart of the wet season and afternoon storms can run longer than the standard 1-2 hours.

## March: Spring Begins, Holi Lights Up Rajasthan, and Cherry Blossoms Forecast Early

March is the best-value month in European cities and the back end of dry season in India and the Caribbean. Spring break (most US dates fall March 8 to April 20 in 2026) creates a price spike in Mexico and the Caribbean during weeks two and three — travel the first or last week for 20-30% savings on those routes.

### Lisbon, Portugal — Europe's Cheapest Spring City Break

[Lisbon](/destinations/lisbon) in March means 18°C (64°F) afternoon highs, empty miradouros, pastel de nata without the 20-minute Pasteis de Belem queue, and round-trip flights from major European cities under $60 on budget carriers. Hotel prices sit 40-50% below summer.

- **Avg temp:** 11-18°C (52-64°F)
- **Daily budget:** $60-100/person
- **What makes March right:** Tram 28 actually has seats. Alfama is quiet enough to hear fado drifting from a window.
- **Insider tip:** Get the Lisboa Card (24h ~$22) — covers unlimited transit plus entry to 30+ attractions including Jeronimos Monastery and Belem Tower. Day-trip to Sintra by train, $5 round trip. Our [Lisbon 3-day itinerary](/blog/lisbon-3-day-itinerary) maps the route. If you're choosing between Lisbon and Porto, our [Lisbon vs Porto comparison](/blog/lisbon-vs-porto) breaks it down.

### Rajasthan, India — Holi Festival, March 3-4

Holi is the festival of colors, the celebration of spring's arrival, and one of travel's genuine bucket-list cultural events. Jaipur, Udaipur, and Jodhpur in March 2026 deliver warm days (18-32°C), clear skies, and the visually richest landscape on the subcontinent — pink City Palace, blue old town, lake palaces, golden sandstone fort.

- **Avg temp:** 18-32°C (64-90°F)
- **Daily budget:** $25-60/person
- **What makes March right:** Holi falls on March 3-4 in 2026. Beyond the festival, March is the tail end of peak season — slightly lower prices than January-February with weather still cool enough for forts.
- **Insider tip:** Wear clothes you don't mind ruining (colored powder is permanent). Book Holi-specific events through your hotel rather than wandering into street celebrations — first-timers find the unfiltered version overwhelming. Heritage hotels (converted havelis and palaces) run $30-60/night for properties that would be $200+ in Europe.

### Cartagena, Colombia — Caribbean Dry Season

Late March in Cartagena is the post-spring-break, pre-rainy-season sweet spot: 28-32°C, near-zero rainfall, water at 27°C. Getsemani has gentrified but still beats the walled city on price-to-atmosphere ratio. A set lunch (almuerzo) at a comedor: $3-5 including soup, rice, protein, salad, and fresh juice.

- **Avg temp:** 28-32°C (82-90°F)
- **Daily budget:** $50-90/person
- **What makes March right:** Reliable Caribbean heat without the December-February holiday surcharge.
- **Insider tip:** Bazurto Market is the actual food scene — chaotic, local, unphotogenic, and cheap. Ceviche stands near the fish section: $2-3. Go before 11 AM.

**Avoid in March:** Tokyo for cherry blossoms unless you're flexible on dates — the 2026 forecast predicts first bloom around March 21 with full bloom (mankai) only by March 28, a 5-7 day window. If sakura is your reason to travel, treat it as an April trip and read the next section.

## April: Sakura, Tulips, and the Mediterranean Before the Crowds

April rewards travelers who can move on a dime around Easter (April 5, 2026 Western, April 12 Orthodox). Easter weekend creates a 25-40% price spike in Catholic Europe; arrive April 13-20 for shoulder-season pricing at otherwise identical conditions. Spring break tails off by mid-April for Caribbean and Mexico routes.

### Tokyo & Kyoto — Cherry Blossom Full Bloom

The reason millions of people travel in April. The 2026 forecast predicts full bloom around March 28 in Tokyo and April 1-2 in Kyoto and Osaka, with petals lingering through April 7-10 in central Japan. After that, chase the bloom north — Sendai peaks around April 11, Sapporo in Hokkaido not until late April. A JR Pass makes the chase economically rational.

- **Avg temp:** 14-20°C (57-68°F) Tokyo; cooler Kyoto
- **Daily budget:** $80-140/person (hotel rates spike 40-60%)
- **What makes April right:** Sakura. Specifically the first ten days. Walk the Meguro River at sunrise. Picnic under the trees in Ueno or Shinjuku Gyoen ($2 entry). Walk Kyoto's Philosopher's Path.
- **Insider tip:** If you arrive after petals have fallen in Tokyo, head north — don't stay and salvage a non-sakura trip. For the full plan, our [Tokyo 4-day itinerary](/blog/tokyo-4-day-itinerary), [first trip to Japan guide](/blog/first-trip-to-japan-what-you-need-to-know), and [cherry blossom season guide](/blog/japan-cherry-blossom-season-guide) cover what you need.

### Amsterdam, Netherlands — Tulip Season Peak

Keukenhof Gardens is open March 19 to May 10, 2026, with the best tulip displays from mid-April onward. The Bloemencorso Flower Parade is on April 18, 2026. Amsterdam shakes off winter with an energy that's palpable along every canal.

- **Avg temp:** 8-14°C (46-57°F)
- **Daily budget:** $100-170/person
- **What makes April right:** The narrow window when the bulb fields surrounding Keukenhof actually look like the marketing photos.
- **Insider tip:** Keukenhof on Bloemencorso day (April 18) sells out months ahead. Visit on a weekday morning instead — the gardens are 60% less crowded before 11 AM. The tulip fields between Leiden and Haarlem are free to view from the road and at peak color in mid-to-late April. [Amsterdam](/destinations/amsterdam) by bicycle ($12-15/day) is the only correct way to see the city in tulip season.

### Lisbon, Portugal — Pre-Season Perfection (Still)

[Lisbon](/destinations/lisbon) carries its March advantages into April with marginally warmer weather and slightly more crowds. Accommodation 30-40% lower than summer, walk-in restaurant tables most evenings, full outdoor terrace season open in Alfama. April is when Lisbon is most underrated — June gets the festival energy, but April gets the price.

- **Avg temp:** 12-19°C (54-66°F)
- **Daily budget:** $70-110/person
- **What makes April right:** Best price-to-conditions ratio in Western Europe.
- **Insider tip:** Skip the tourist-priced restaurants in Alfama. Walk 10 minutes to Mouraria — Lisbon's most multicultural neighborhood — for meals at half the price.

**Avoid in April:** Bali during Nyepi (Day of Silence, falls in late March or sometimes early April depending on the lunar calendar). The entire island shuts down — no flights into Denpasar, no internet, no leaving your accommodation. Confirm dates before booking.

## May: Shoulder Season Europe, Inca Trail Dry Season, and the Last Window Before Summer Prices Hit

May is the month travel professionals keep for themselves. European cities are warm, green, and 20-30% cheaper than June. The Mediterranean is open but not yet overrun. Machu Picchu enters its dry season on May 1.

### Amalfi Coast, Italy — Before the Crowds and the Heat

The Amalfi Coast in July is one of the most congested stretches of road in Europe. In May, lemon trees are heavy with fruit, sea is turquoise, terrace restaurants are open without 45-minute waits, and accommodation is 25-40% lower than peak. Water reaches 19-20°C by late May — swimmable for most people.

- **Avg temp:** 18-24°C (64-75°F)
- **Daily budget:** EUR 90-150/person
- **What makes May right:** Path of the Gods (Sentiero degli Dei) covered in wildflowers. Ferries between Positano, Amalfi, and Ravello at full schedule but not at gridlock.
- **Insider tip:** Skip Positano accommodation — premium price for the name. Stay in Praiano or Furore: 10-20 minutes by ferry, 30-40% less. Our [5-day Italy itinerary](/blog/5-day-italy-itinerary) pairs the coast with [Rome](/destinations/rome) and Florence.

### Marrakech, Morocco — Last Window Before the Summer Furnace

Marrakech hits 38-42°C from June through September. May runs 18-28°C — warm enough for outdoor dining at Djemaa el-Fna without the heat that drains you by 10 AM in summer. Sits between the Easter and summer tourist surges, so prices are at their spring low. Ramadan ends April 30 in 2026, so May is fully post-Ramadan with all restaurants open.

- **Avg temp:** 18-28°C (64-82°F)
- **Daily budget:** EUR 35-70/person
- **What makes May right:** The same medina, riads, and souks at low-season pricing without the heat penalty.
- **Insider tip:** The souks are a negotiation gym. Start at 25-30% of the asking price. Best leather, spices, and ceramics live in the secondary alleys off the main tourist routes.

### Peru — Machu Picchu's Dry Season Opens May 1

May 1 is the start of the four best months for the Inca Trail and surrounding Andes. Rain is rare, ruins are cloud-free, mountain views are clear. May permit availability beats July-August by a meaningful margin — booking 3-4 months ahead in May still works; in July it doesn't.

- **Avg temp:** 14-20°C (57-68°F) Cusco, warmer at lower elevations
- **Daily budget:** EUR 55-100/person
- **What makes May right:** Same conditions as the peak July-August window with a fraction of the people and most of the permit availability.
- **Insider tip:** Build in 2 days acclimatizing in Cusco (3,400 m). Don't fly in and trek out the next day. If the Classic Inca Trail is sold out, the Salkantay Trek delivers comparable scenery with no permit system.

**Avoid in May:** Sri Lanka's southwest coast (Colombo, Galle, Mirissa) — the southwest monsoon arrives in May. Flip to the east coast (Trincomalee, Arugam Bay) instead, where dry season starts in May and runs through September.

## June: Midnight Sun, Mediterranean Sweet Spot, and the Last of Shoulder-Season Pricing

June is the traveler's golden window. Northern Hemisphere temperatures are warm but not scorching, daylight stretches past 9 PM in southern Europe and never fully disappears up north, and mass tourism hasn't crested. The first half of the month is still shoulder pricing in most of Europe; school holidays kick in around June 20 and prices follow.

### Iceland & Lofoten, Norway — Midnight Sun at Its Most Useful

The summer solstice on June 21 delivers nearly 24 hours of daylight. Iceland's highland F-roads open in mid-June. Lofoten gets its dramatic Arctic light from mid-June through mid-July: jagged granite peaks rising from turquoise water, fishing villages painted red and yellow.

- **Avg temp:** 12°C (54°F) Iceland; 14°C (57°F) Lofoten — but sunny days push to 20-25°C
- **Daily budget:** $150-280/person
- **What makes June right:** Highland roads finally open. Puffins arrive and nest. Whales feed in northern bays. June is also Iceland's driest month statistically.
- **Insider tip:** Campervans in Iceland combine accommodation and transport at $110-160/day in June. Booked in February: 30-40% cheaper than booked in May. Lofoten — book a rorbu (converted fisherman's cabin) in Hamnoy or Sakrisøy. For broader options, our [midnight sun destinations guide](/blog/midnight-sun-best-destinations) covers the alternatives.

### Lisbon, Portugal — Festas de Lisboa and the Festival Energy

The Feast of Saint Anthony on June 12-13 transforms Lisbon into an open-air festival: sardines grilling on every corner, neighborhood marchas populares parading through Avenida da Liberdade. EuroPride Lisbon takes over from June 14-23. MEO Kalorama runs June 19-21. Eleven hours of daily sunshine and 25°C makes the city the best version of itself.

- **Avg temp:** 25°C (77°F) high, 16°C (61°F) low
- **Daily budget:** $100-150/person
- **What makes June right:** The most authentic Lisbon experience available — and weather to match.
- **Insider tip:** Out Jazz launches in June — free jazz and soul concerts in public gardens every Sunday through September. Combine with a [3-day Lisbon plan](/blog/lisbon-3-day-itinerary). If you're choosing Lisbon over Porto for a city break, our [Lisbon vs Porto comparison](/blog/lisbon-vs-porto) addresses the trade-offs.

### Tokyo, Japan — Off-Season Hydrangeas and the Sanno Matsuri

June is Tokyo's off-season — the rainy season (tsuyu) begins in early June, but it doesn't mean constant downpours. Expect intermittent drizzles mixed with clear spells. The first half is typically drier. In return: hotel prices 30-40% below peak, shorter queues everywhere, and one of Tokyo's most beautiful seasonal displays — hydrangeas blooming across temples and parks.

- **Avg temp:** 25°C (77°F) high, 18°C (64°F) low
- **Daily budget:** $100-160/person
- **What makes June right:** The Sanno Matsuri — one of Tokyo's three great festivals — runs June 7-17 in 2026. It only happens in even-numbered years. Main procession on June 15 winds through central Tokyo with 500+ Heian-period costumes.
- **Insider tip:** [Tokyo](/destinations/tokyo) hotels that cost $200/night in October drop to $130-150 in June. Hakusan Shrine and Asukayama Park are peak hydrangea spots, early-to-mid June.

### Santorini, Greece — Cleanest Caldera Weather of the Year

Aegean is warm (22°C), sky is cloudless, the iconic white villages glow against the blue. June is the start of high season but isn't yet at July-August saturation. Watch the sunset from Akrotiri Lighthouse rather than Oia — same view, 30 people instead of 3,000.

- **Avg temp:** 27°C (81°F) high
- **Daily budget:** $150-220/person
- **What makes June right:** Peak conditions, sub-peak crowds.
- **Insider tip:** Stay in Perissa or Kamari ($60-90/night) rather than Oia or Fira ($150-300+). Rent an ATV ($25-35/day).

**Avoid in June:** Cusco unless you've already secured Inca Trail permits — June books out 6 months ahead. The Salkantay Trek is the move if you're improvising. Also avoid Tokyo if rain ruins your trip — the first half is drier than the second, but there are no guarantees.

## July: Peak Season, Festival Season, and the Cool-Escape Strategy

July is the most popular month for international travel globally, which means peak prices and peak crowds in obvious destinations. The smarter move is targeting destinations that genuinely peak in July — events that don't happen any other month — or escaping the heat entirely. Book 4-6 months ahead, period.

### Algarve, Portugal — Western Europe's Most Reliable Beach Weather

The Algarve gets 12 hours of sunshine per day in July with virtually zero rain. The Atlantic at 21°C is cooler than the Mediterranean — refreshing rather than bathwater. Dramatic sandstone cliffs, sea caves at Benagil, hidden coves, all photogenic under high summer sun. Significantly cheaper than the French Riviera or Amalfi Coast.

- **Avg temp:** 29°C (84°F) high, 20°C (68°F) low
- **Daily budget:** $100-160/person
- **What makes July right:** Cliff scenery, swimmable Atlantic, and the cataplana seafood stew is at peak.
- **Insider tip:** Skip overcrowded Benagil Cave boat tours — rent a kayak ($15/hour) and paddle in at your own pace. Go before 10 AM when the cave's interior light is best.

### Masai Mara, Kenya — The Great Migration Begins

Every July, roughly 1.5 million wildebeest, 500,000 zebras, and 200,000 gazelles pour from the Serengeti into Kenya's Masai Mara. Predators follow. The Mara River crossings are the most dramatic wildlife spectacle on Earth. This is not a generic safari — it's the single greatest wildlife event on the planet, and July is when it begins.

- **Avg temp:** 24°C (75°F) day, 12°C (54°F) night
- **Daily budget:** $150-350/person
- **What makes July right:** River crossings begin. Cool dry season means clear skies and good visibility for photography.
- **Insider tip:** Stay in a private conservancy bordering the reserve — Ol Kinyei, Naboisho, Olare Motorogi. Same migration herds, 90% fewer vehicles, and night drives and walking safaris that the main reserve prohibits. For broader safari timing, our [Great Migration guide](/blog/great-migration-africa-when-and-where) covers the full year.

### Iceland — Ring Road Fully Open, Puffin Colonies at Peak

July is the only month every section of Route 1 (1,322 km) is reliably open. Puffins are at peak activity along the coasts. 20-21 hours of functional daylight. The warmest month — though "warm" still means a jacket.

- **Avg temp:** 13°C (55°F)
- **Daily budget:** $180-280/person
- **What makes July right:** Highland F-roads at peak access, full ferry schedule, and the brief window when every campground and trailhead is operational.
- **Insider tip:** Skip the Blue Lagoon ($75-100). Free hot springs deliver the same experience: Reykjadalur (45-minute hike, free), Seljavallalaug (hidden mountain pool, free).

### Bali, Indonesia — Dry Season at Its Cleanest

July sits in the heart of [Bali's](/destinations/bali) dry season. Consistent sunshine, low humidity, clean offshore swells on the west coast. The Bali Arts Festival runs through mid-July at the Taman Werdhi Budaya art center in Denpasar. Slightly more expensive than shoulder months but still extraordinary value.

- **Avg temp:** 28°C (82°F) high
- **Daily budget:** $50-85/person
- **What makes July right:** The driest month of the year, period.
- **Insider tip:** Split the trip between coasts. West (Canggu, Uluwatu) for offshore-wind surf. East (Amed, Tulamben) for calm-water diving — the USAT Liberty wreck at Tulamben is accessible from shore. Our [Bali 7-day itinerary](/blog/bali-7-day-itinerary) covers the route. If you're choosing between Bali and Thailand, our [Bali vs Thailand comparison](/blog/bali-vs-thailand) addresses the trade-offs directly.

**Avoid in July:** Santorini unless price doesn't matter (July is the most expensive month, with caldera-view rooms at $300-500+). Marrakech unless your riad has a plunge pool (37-40°C+ daytime). Rome and Athens — Mediterranean cities running 35-38°C with no reprieve.

## August: Festivals, Migration River Crossings, and Why You Should Go South Instead

August is when most of the world travels at once. Mediterranean resorts hit their annual peak: overpriced, overheated, overbooked. The smart move is to go where August does something specific and irreplicable — Edinburgh's festivals, the Serengeti river crossings, southern hemisphere winter — rather than fighting for an Amalfi reservation.

### Edinburgh, Scotland — The Largest Arts Festival on the Planet

The Edinburgh Festival Fringe runs all of August: 3,400+ shows across 300+ venues, plus the Edinburgh International Festival, the Military Tattoo, and the Art, Book, and Film Festivals running simultaneously. Edinburgh in August is a city that has lost its mind in the best possible way.

- **Avg temp:** 19°C (66°F) high, 12°C (55°F) low
- **Daily budget:** $130-200/person
- **What makes August right:** The festivals only happen in August. Three or four shows per day is achievable. Tickets range from free (the "Free Fringe") to $30, with the best comedy and drama selling out weeks ahead.
- **Insider tip:** PBH's Free Fringe and Laughing Horse listings include some of the festival's best acts on a pass-the-bucket model. You can do three or four excellent shows in a day without spending a penny.

### Slovenia & Montenegro — The Smart Adriatic Alternatives

Slovenia is what travelers want when they book Croatia in August and find it overrun. Lake Bled is postcard-perfect at 26°C with the lake hitting 22°C — alpine swimming weather. Triglav National Park and the Soča Valley deliver serious hiking and turquoise-water canyoning. Ljubljana is one of Europe's most charming small capitals.

Montenegro is what Dubrovnik used to be. Kotor's UNESCO Old Town is 30 minutes from Dubrovnik airport with a fraction of the crowds. The Bay of Kotor is the Mediterranean's southernmost fjord. Not yet in the EU, which keeps prices at the level Croatia had a decade ago.

- **Avg temp:** Slovenia 26°C, Montenegro 30°C
- **Daily budget:** $80-140/person
- **What makes August right:** Same coastline drama and lake-and-mountain country, none of the Croatian saturation.
- **Insider tip:** Stay in Ribčev Laz on Lake Bled's eastern shore rather than Bled town — better views, quieter, 15-20% cheaper. Climb Kotor's city walls at 7 AM before August heat makes them grueling.

### Serengeti, Tanzania — Peak River Crossing Window

If July's Masai Mara was the migration's beginning, August is its biggest crossing month. Hundreds of thousands of wildebeest plunge across the crocodile-filled Mara River. It is not a budget safari, but as a first Africa experience, nothing else compares.

- **Avg temp:** 25°C (77°F)
- **Daily budget:** $200-500/person
- **What makes August right:** Peak crossing activity.
- **Insider tip:** Conservancies bordering the main parks offer the same wildlife with night drives and walking safaris that public parks prohibit. See our [Great Migration guide](/blog/great-migration-africa-when-and-where) for the year-by-year route.

**Avoid in August:** The entire Mediterranean coast unless you have already paid. Santorini, Dubrovnik, Amalfi, and the Greek Islands all run their most expensive month with their highest crowds. If you must do the Mediterranean in August, choose Montenegro or Slovenia.

## September: Shoulder-Season Europe at Its Absolute Peak

September is, on the math, the single best month for European travel. Mediterranean cities run 24-28°C, summer crowds dispersed almost overnight after schools restart, hotels drop 30-45% from August, and flight prices crater. The exception: anywhere with a major September festival, where prices stay peak.

### Barcelona, Spain — La Merce, September 20-24

La Merce is [Barcelona's](/destinations/barcelona) biggest and most spectacular festival: free concerts in plazas, human towers (castells), fire runs (correfocs) through the Gothic Quarter, and a fireworks show over Montjuïc. It's the one week the city feels like it belongs entirely to its residents. Mediterranean water at 25°C, hotels 30-40% cheaper than August.

- **Avg temp:** 26°C (79°F)
- **Daily budget:** $110-170/person
- **What makes September right:** La Merce is genuinely citywide and genuinely free. Sagrada Familia tickets available 2-3 weeks ahead instead of months.
- **Insider tip:** Time the trip around La Merce. The correfoc through the Gothic Quarter alone justifies the trip. Beach overflow to Bogatell or Mar Bella — same water, a quarter of the people. Our [Barcelona 3-day itinerary](/blog/barcelona-3-day-itinerary) maps the rest. If you're deciding between Barcelona and another city, our [Paris vs Barcelona comparison](/blog/paris-vs-barcelona) addresses the trade-offs.

### Istanbul, Turkey — Best-Value City Break in Europe

September in [Istanbul](/destinations/istanbul) is a masterclass in shoulder-season value. The 35°C+ summer humidity drops to a comfortable 25°C. Grand Bazaar is busy but navigable. Lira remains favorable. A sit-down dinner for two at a quality restaurant: $20-30.

- **Avg temp:** 25°C (77°F)
- **Daily budget:** $60-100/person
- **What makes September right:** Peak weather and post-peak crowds. Bosphorus light is exceptional.
- **Insider tip:** Cross the Galata Bridge at sunset, eat a balik ekmek (fish sandwich, $4) from the boats below, then walk up to Galata Tower for 360-degree views of the city in twilight. Our [Istanbul 3-day itinerary](/blog/istanbul-3-day-itinerary) covers the route.

### Bali, Indonesia — Dry Season, Pre-Crowd

September is the tail end of [Bali's](/destinations/bali) dry season. The November-March rain hasn't started, humidity is lower than July-August, and Australian school holiday crowds (Bali's largest segment) have gone home. Prices for villas drop from the second week onward.

- **Avg temp:** 28°C (82°F)
- **Daily budget:** $50-90/person
- **What makes September right:** Possibly the single best month to visit Bali on a price-to-conditions basis.
- **Insider tip:** Diving visibility off Amed exceeds 25 meters in September. Sidemen for rice-terrace views without Ubud's traffic. Munduk in the north for cooler mountain temperatures and waterfalls.

**Avoid in September:** Caribbean and Gulf Coast destinations — September is the statistical peak of Atlantic hurricane season. Travel insurance is mandatory if you go.

## October: Fall Foliage, Oktoberfest, and Mediterranean Late Summer

October is the month that rewards travelers who pay attention to timing. Foliage windows shift week-to-week, Oktoberfest 2026 actually ends October 4 (Munich pricing drops 30-40% the next day), and Mediterranean destinations remain swimmable through mid-October. Flights from the U.S. to Europe average $400-550 round-trip versus $700-1,000 in summer.

### New England, USA — The Planet's Best Free Show

The combination of sugar maples, red oaks, birches, and aspens creates a color palette no other region matches. The first two weeks of October are peak foliage across Vermont and New Hampshire; Massachusetts and Connecticut peak mid-to-late October. The progression moves south and downhill, giving a 3-4 week window if you chase color.

- **Avg temp:** 12-16°C (54-61°F) high, 2-6°C (36-43°F) low
- **Daily budget:** $150-250/person
- **What makes October right:** Foliage. There is nowhere else on the calendar this delivers.
- **Insider tip:** Skip weekend drives — Saturday traffic on Vermont's Route 100 turns a 2-hour drive into 4. Midweek is dramatically less crowded. Use weekly New England foliage tracker maps to time the visit precisely. Our [fall foliage guide](/blog/best-fall-foliage-destinations) covers the full set of options.

### Crete, Greece — Late-Summer Mediterranean

October Crete is Greece's best-kept shoulder season. Aegean still warm at 23°C, air at 23°C without summer's 35°C+, full tourist infrastructure but a fraction of visitors, hotels 40-50% below peak. The southern coast (Paleochora, Plakias) stays warmer and drier longer.

- **Avg temp:** 23°C (73°F)
- **Daily budget:** $80-130/person
- **What makes October right:** Beach weather without summer prices, and the Samaria Gorge hike at temperatures that don't break you.
- **Insider tip:** The Tsikoudia (raki) Festival happens in various villages across Crete throughout October — locals distill fresh raki from the grape harvest. Ask your accommodation host for the nearest one.

### Marrakech, Morocco — Annual Weather Sweet Spot

[Marrakech](/destinations/marrakech) in October hits its weather peak. Summer's 40°C+ has mellowed to 27°C. Rain is essentially nonexistent — fewer than 2 rainy days. Markets, riads, and street food at full capacity with significantly fewer visitors than spring.

- **Avg temp:** 27°C (81°F)
- **Daily budget:** $50-80/person
- **What makes October right:** The single most comfortable medina-walking weather of the year.
- **Insider tip:** Date harvest arrives in October. The dried fruit and nut stalls in the medina sell the freshest Medjool dates of the year — completely different from the packaged international versions.

### Munich, Germany — Oktoberfest's Final Days, Then Normalcy

Oktoberfest 2026 runs September 19 to October 4. The final weekend (October 2-4) has a specific energy — part celebration, part farewell. After October 4, hotel rates drop 30-40% overnight and Munich returns to standard Bavarian autumn beauty.

- **Avg temp:** 12°C (54°F)
- **Daily budget:** $120-180/person during Oktoberfest; $100-150 after
- **What makes October right:** Festival's end has the best atmosphere with the lowest crowds — and the post-festival arrival captures all of Munich at standard prices.
- **Insider tip:** Tuesday after the first Oktoberfest weekend is Italian Day (traditionally discounted). The final Sunday's "Oktoberfest riflemen's salute" at noon (a 21-gun salute) signals the festival's end.

**Avoid in October:** Anywhere expecting Caribbean stability — late hurricane season risk continues through October.

## November: Tokyo Foliage, Pre-Holiday Tropics, and Europe's Quietest Month

November is the travel calendar's best-kept secret. Summer crowds are a memory, December holiday surge hasn't started, flights drop ~30% from peak, and hotels across Europe and Asia slash rates aggressively. November requires precision — some destinations are sliding into rainy or cold seasons. The right places are at their absolute peak.

### Tokyo, Japan — Koyo Foliage Season

November is peak autumn foliage in [Tokyo](/destinations/tokyo). Japanese take koyo (autumn leaf viewing) as seriously as cherry blossoms. Parks and gardens turn crimson, gold, and amber while temperatures hover in the comfortable mid-teens. Unlike sakura madness, November crowds are manageable. Reliably dry with crisp blue skies. This is Tokyo at its most photogenic without the chaos.

- **Avg temp:** 10-18°C (50-64°F)
- **Daily budget:** $100-180/person
- **What makes November right:** Rikugien Gardens and Koishikawa Korakuen run evening illumination events in mid-to-late November that turn foliage into something otherworldly. Meiji Jingu Gaien's ginkgo avenue turns blazing yellow.
- **Insider tip:** Day-trip to Nikko (2 hours north by train) where autumn color is even more spectacular than Tokyo, framing shrines and waterfalls. Weekday only — domestic crowds on weekends are heavy. Our [Tokyo 4-day itinerary](/blog/tokyo-4-day-itinerary) covers core logistics.

### Sri Lanka — East Coast Dry Season, Cultural Triangle Open

November sees Sri Lanka's east and north coasts entering their dry season — Trincomalee, Arugam Bay, Passikudah. November is also the cheapest month before the December-March high season. The Cultural Triangle (Sigiriya, Dambulla, Polonnaruwa) is comfortable to explore, and November is whale-watching season off Trincomalee's coast (blue whales and sperm whales).

- **Avg temp:** 25-30°C (77-86°F)
- **Daily budget:** $40-90/person
- **What makes November right:** Compact island, two coastlines on opposite monsoon schedules — flip sides in 3-4 hours if you need to.
- **Insider tip:** Sigiriya Rock Fortress is best climbed early morning. November's softer light makes the ancient frescoes and the panoramic view from the summit particularly memorable. Whale-watching boats from Trincomalee: $40-60 per person, success rates genuinely high.

### Lisbon, Portugal — Off-Season Value Capital of Europe

[Lisbon](/destinations/lisbon) in November is Europe's best shoulder-season value. Summer's intensity gone, mild walking weather, hotel prices 30-50% below peak. November rain is real (6-8 rainy days) but showers tend to be intermittent, not all-day. Soft autumn light makes the azulejo tile work glow.

- **Avg temp:** 11-18°C (52-64°F)
- **Daily budget:** $80-140/person
- **What makes November right:** Mid-range boutique hotels in Alfama or Bairro Alto that charge $180-250 in July drop to $80-120.
- **Insider tip:** Feira da Ladra flea market (Tuesdays and Saturdays) — Lisbon's antique-and-oddity market since the 12th century. Smaller crowds in November mean better bargaining leverage. Arrive before 10 AM. Our [Lisbon 3-day itinerary](/blog/lisbon-3-day-itinerary) covers a full plan.

**Avoid in November:** The entire western Caribbean if your trip overlaps with Thanksgiving — domestic US travel chaos extends to outbound flights, and the Sunday after Thanksgiving is the year's worst flying day.

## December: Christmas Markets, Ski-Season Opens, and Southern Hemisphere Summer

December is the most polarizing month on the calendar. Generic "somewhere warm" searches end in overpriced resorts. The trick is going where December does something specific — Christmas markets, dry-season tropics, southern hemisphere summer, ski openings — rather than fighting for a non-specific tropical week. The single biggest savings lever is avoiding December 20 to January 2 entirely.

### Vienna & Prague — The Real Christmas Market Cities

[Vienna](/destinations/vienna) doesn't just do Christmas markets — it turns the entire city into a coordinated holiday production. Over 20 markets across the city, each with a different character. Rathausplatz is the headliner; Spittelberg has handmade crafts in narrow Biedermeier lanes; the Christkindlmarkt at Belvedere Palace uses the Upper Belvedere as a backdrop.

[Prague](/destinations/prague) is the affordable counterpart. Old Town Square and Wenceslas Square markets are genuinely good — local vendors selling Bohemian glass alongside the expected mulled wine. December is the only time medieval spires, fresh snow on cobblestones, and trdelnik stands hit a very specific aesthetic nerve.

- **Avg temp:** Vienna 1-4°C; Prague -1 to 3°C
- **Daily budget:** $130-200 Vienna; $80-130 Prague
- **What makes December right:** Christmas markets only happen now, and they're the real reason to be in Central Europe in the cold.
- **Insider tip:** Vienna 24-hour transit pass: EUR 8 covering trams, metro, buses across the city. Buy a Punschkrapfen (punch cake) for EUR 3 instead of the EUR 5-6 tourist Glühwein. Prague: the Tyl Market at Náměstí Republiky opens later and is best for evening visits.

### Bangkok, Thailand — Best Weather of the Year

[Bangkok](/destinations/bangkok) in December delivers the city's best weather: 28-31°C during the day, almost zero rain, lower humidity than the brutal March-May period. The monsoon is over, streets are dry, outdoor dining and temple visits are genuinely comfortable. New Year's Eve along the Chao Phraya River with fireworks over Wat Arun is one of Asia's best countdowns.

- **Avg temp:** 22-31°C (72-88°F)
- **Daily budget:** $40-110/person
- **What makes December right:** The single most reliable weather window of the year for this city. Pad Thai from a Michelin-recognized street vendor: $1.50-2.
- **Insider tip:** Chatuchak Weekend Market (15,000+ stalls) is browseable in December, unlike the brutal heat of summer. Saturday morning before 10 AM. Our [Bangkok 5-day itinerary](/blog/bangkok-5-day-itinerary) covers a full plan.

### Marrakech, Morocco — Mild-Weather Outlier

[Marrakech](/destinations/marrakech) in December sits in a mild sweet spot — warm enough for daytime medina wandering (18-22°C), cool enough to actually walk for hours. Rainfall is minimal. Tourism is low enough that you'll get better prices on everything from riads to guided tours.

- **Avg temp:** 9-20°C (48-68°F)
- **Daily budget:** $50-120/person
- **What makes December right:** While the rest of North Africa and the Mediterranean are too cold for outdoor exploration, Marrakech is at its most comfortable.
- **Insider tip:** December evenings drop to 7-9°C. The rooftop terraces that are essential in summer become less appealing at night. Book a traditional Moroccan dinner inside a riad ($15-25 per person) — tagine, couscous, mint tea by candlelight.

**Avoid in December:** Anywhere on the calendar from December 20 to January 2 if you can shift dates. This 13-day window carries the highest prices of the entire year globally. December 1-15 offers similar weather at 30-50% lower cost in most destinations.

## Picking the Right Month for Your Trip

If you've made it this far, you have the calendar. The harder question is which slot is yours. Most readers don't actually need a month — they need to work backward from one constraint. Here's how I'd narrow it down.

**Start with the constraint that's least flexible.** For most people that's either dates (you have a fixed week of vacation) or budget. If your dates are fixed, the calendar above is your menu — pick from your month. If budget is fixed and dates are open, work the second column of the quick-pick table. The shoulder-season picks for January (Marrakech), May (Marrakech again), and November (Lisbon) deliver the most experience per dollar in the entire year.

**If "warmest beach" is the goal:** Maldives in February (peak dry season, 25-30 m visibility), Bali in July (driest month), Crete in late September (water still 23°C, prices crashed), or Bangkok in December (28-31°C, near-zero rain). Anything described as "warm and dry" outside these windows requires more research than a quick-pick deserves.

**If "biggest event" is the goal:** Rio Carnival February 13-18, 2026 (book yesterday). Sakura full bloom April 1-7 in Kyoto and Osaka (book by January). Inti Raymi June 24 in Cusco (Inca Trail permits sell out 6 months ahead). Edinburgh Fringe all of August (book accommodation 6 months ahead). La Merce Barcelona September 20-24. Day of the Dead Oaxaca November 1-2. The pattern: events with hard dates require booking on hard timelines. If you're reading this in May 2026, Inti Raymi is still doable, Edinburgh Fringe needs urgent action, and Carnival 2027 is something to start thinking about now.

**If "lowest cost" is the goal:** February (statistically the cheapest month to fly globally outside Carnival/Valentine's windows), early November before Black Friday, and the first two weeks of December before the 20th. Within those months: Marrakech ($40-100/day), Bangkok ($40-110/day), Bali ($40-90/day), Istanbul ($45-100/day) consistently come in lowest. Egypt in February ($50-120/day) is the value outlier — extraordinary historical density at near-Asia pricing.

**If "specific natural phenomenon" is the goal:** Northern Lights — Lapland in January or February during the current solar maximum, while it lasts. Midnight sun — Iceland or Lofoten in late June. Cherry blossoms — Tokyo and Kyoto in early April. New England fall foliage — first two weeks of October. Great Migration river crossings — Masai Mara in late July and August, Serengeti through August. Whale season — Hermanus in August, Trincomalee in November, the Azores in May. None of these are interchangeable. Match the trip to the phenomenon, not the other way around.

One more thing. If you're choosing between two months because you can only go in one of them, the right answer is almost always the shoulder month — May over June, September over August, October over July. Same conditions, 25-40% cheaper, and the version of the destination you'd actually want to come back to.

## FAQ

### What's the cheapest month to travel internationally in 2026?

February, on the math. US, European, and Australian airlines consistently post their lowest fares in February outside of Carnival weekends (Rio runs February 13-18, 2026) and Valentine's week (February 9-16). January is a close second once the post-holiday surge clears around January 7-10. The smartest specific window is February 17-28 — the Maldives, Egypt, Thailand, and most non-Carnival destinations all run their lowest pricing of the year in that 12-day stretch. November before Black Friday and the first two weeks of December before December 20 round out the cheapest periods.

### When is shoulder season in Europe?

There are two shoulder windows in Europe and they aren't equivalent. The spring shoulder runs roughly April through mid-May (after Easter, before school holidays kick in around May 20). The fall shoulder runs mid-September through October. September is statistically the better of the two — Mediterranean water is still warm at 24-25°C, prices have dropped 30-45% from August, and the festival calendar (La Merce, Oktoberfest's start, the Venice Film Festival) is dense. October extends the value into Mediterranean late summer for cities like Rome, Crete, and Marrakech. May is the better spring window for cooler-water destinations (Algarve, Greek Islands, Provence) and for shoulder pricing across the board. April is shoulder only outside Easter weekend (April 3-6, 2026 Western; April 10-13 Orthodox).

### Best month for cherry blossoms in 2026?

Late March through early April for central Japan, with regional variation that matters. The 2026 forecast from the Japan Meteorological Corporation predicts: Tokyo first bloom around March 21, full bloom (mankai) around March 28, petals lingering through April 7-10. Kyoto and Osaka full bloom April 1-2. Sendai full bloom around April 11. Sapporo not until late April. If you're planning a trip specifically for sakura, target arrival March 28 to April 5 in Tokyo or Kyoto, and have a JR Pass ready to chase the bloom north if you arrive late. Outside Japan, Paris and Amsterdam both have cherry blossoms in parks during the first two weeks of April, though displays are smaller. Our [cherry blossom season guide](/blog/japan-cherry-blossom-season-guide) covers timing in detail.

### Worst month for Southeast Asia weather?

It depends on which part of Southeast Asia. The general southwest monsoon affects Thailand's Andaman coast (Phuket, Krabi), Malaysia's west coast, and most of mainland Southeast Asia from May through October — peak rainfall typically in September. Bali's wet season runs November through March, with January and February the heaviest. Sri Lanka is the case where the country has two monsoons on opposite schedules — the southwest coast (Colombo, Galle) is monsoon May-August; the east coast (Trincomalee, Arugam Bay) is dry May-September. The blanket "worst" month for the broadest definition of Southeast Asia is September — peak southwest monsoon, peak typhoon risk for the Philippines and southern Japan. Our [monsoon season guide](/blog/monsoon-season-where-to-go-and-avoid) breaks the regional patterns down. The blanket "best" month for the same broad definition is December or January — almost everywhere is in dry season.

### When does Easter fall in 2026 and how does it affect prices?

Easter Sunday is April 5, 2026 (Western) and April 12, 2026 (Orthodox). Western Easter creates a 25-40% price spike in Catholic and Protestant Europe (Rome, Paris, Barcelona, Lisbon) for the long weekend April 3-6. Orthodox Easter creates a similar spike in Greece, Croatia, and Eastern Europe for April 10-13 — Santorini and Dubrovnik in particular. The strategic move if Easter isn't your reason to travel: arrive April 13-20. You get identical spring weather at shoulder-season prices and crowds thin noticeably.

### When should I book a 2026 trip?

Long-haul flights: 4-6 months ahead is the consistent sweet spot for transatlantic and trans-Pacific routes. Short-haul European: 6-10 weeks. Caribbean and Mexico in winter: 3-4 months ahead, longer for Christmas/spring break weeks. Specific exceptions that need more lead time: Inca Trail permits (6 months for July-August), Iceland campervans (3 months for June-July), Sambadrome tickets for Rio Carnival (3-4 months minimum), Edinburgh Fringe accommodation (4-6 months), Maldives resort weeks (3 months), Lapland aurora cabins for December-January (3-4 months). Specific exceptions with shorter windows: shoulder-season city breaks (Lisbon, Istanbul, Marrakech) work fine at 2-4 weeks ahead, and last-minute December availability for those same destinations is usually findable.

### Is the solar-maximum aurora season really worth the trip in 2026?

Yes, with caveats. Solar Cycle 25 peaked around late 2024 to early 2025 and elevated activity continues through 2026, with declining intensity year over year. February 2026 is statistically still strong — Finnish Lapland aurora probability runs 70-80% on clear nights, which is the variable that matters more than solar activity itself. The trip is worth taking now rather than later because by 2028-2029, solar minimum will return and aurora frequency will drop noticeably. Within 2026, February delivers the best balance of long dark nights and reasonable daytime activity windows; January is darker but colder; March has more daylight but shorter aurora windows. Inari or Saariselkä, not Rovaniemi.

### Should I buy travel insurance for 2026?

For trips outside hurricane and typhoon seasons, it's optional. For trips overlapping those windows — Caribbean/Gulf Coast May through November, Japan and Philippines August through October, Atlantic crossings July through October — it's mandatory. Policies covering trip interruption and flight delays typically run 5-8% of total trip spend. The single year where I'd insist on insurance regardless of destination is when long-haul flights are booked 4+ months ahead and the trip cost is significant (anything over $3,000 per person). The cost of insurance is dwarfed by what one cancelled flight in peak season can do to a non-refundable booking.
